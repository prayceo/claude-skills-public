# CEO Council

An elite panel of 9 fictional executive advisors representing the full decision surface of a CEO.
Each brings a distinct philosophy and domain expertise. The odd number ensures no voting ties.

## Core Theme

**Clarity before commitment. Challenge before capital.**

**Guiding Philosophy (Gatena Leadership):** *"Mission first, people always."* Every Council debate is grounded in servant leadership: the Council exists to serve the mission (inspiring the world to pray) and the people the mission serves. Members push back when ego, optics, or short-term urgency start crowding out mission or people.

## When to Convene

### Required (Non-Negotiable)
- Capital allocation decisions above a material threshold
- Leadership-level hiring or firing
- New partnerships or distribution deals
- Pricing changes to core products
- Any decision touching the company's faith-mission boundary
- Product pivots or platform bets

### Recommended
- Trade-offs between competing strategic priorities
- Fundraising strategy and investor narrative
- New market entry or audience expansion
- Org restructuring or team design
- Decisions with significant 12-month irreversibility

### Not Required
- Day-to-day operational choices with clear owners
- Tactical execution within an already-approved strategy
- Decisions fully delegated to a capable team member

## How It Works

1. **Pre-Council Diagnostic (Hormozi-style framing)** — Before convening, run the 4 diagnostic questions below. This surfaces whether the decision is even framed correctly, and sharpens what the council is actually being asked to vote on.
2. **Present the decision** — State the question clearly, with relevant context (numbers, timeline, alternatives considered)
3. **Each member responds** — From their unique domain perspective, with their core concern surfaced. Every quantitative claim must include a specific number, not a directional adjective.
4. **Mandatory Transcendent There check** — Every member must answer: *Does this decision move us closer to or further from the 3-year vision?*
5. **Vote** — All 9 members must vote (see VOTING.md)
6. **Synthesis** — Identify agreements, conditions, and the sharpest dissent
7. **Decision** — Majority (5+) wins; conditions must be addressed before execution
8. **Board-Ready Presentation** *(when the decision will be presented to the board, investors, or press)* — Name which look from `looks-taxonomy.md` the decision or its supporting data should become (e.g. "this is an Executive Growth Narrative, not a Financial Operating Table"). Marco Narrative owns this call when he's in the council; otherwise Warren Margin or Magnus Longview flags it before the decision leaves the room.

## Pre-Council Diagnostic

Run these 6 questions *before* convening the council. They come from two sources: the Hormozi diagnostic bias (most decisions fail because they were framed wrong, not debated wrong) and the Gatena Leadership system (most decisions fail because they were motivated wrong — reactive instead of proactive).

**1. What is the actual constraint?** (Theory of Constraints — Hormozi)
> "A business grows to its nearest constraint and no further."

Before evaluating *this* decision, identify the single constraint in the system today. Is this decision addressing the constraint, or a non-constraint? Adding resources to a non-constraint creates waste without throughput. If the council is voting on a decision that doesn't touch the current constraint, that's the first thing to surface.

**2. Is this a More / Better / New problem?** (Marketing Workshop — Hormozi)
- **MORE** → Volume of what already works (the right answer 99% of the time under $50M)
- **BETTER** → Leverage improvements that emerge naturally from More
- **NEW** → Only justified when the market is maxed, the platform is shrinking, or there's a skill mismatch

Most "we need a new product / new vertical / new channel" decisions are *actually* More problems in disguise. Force the question: have we honestly executed the More of what we already have before reaching for New?

**3. What is the Ask?** (ACQ Executive Memo rule — Hormozi/Sharran)
State the Ask in one sentence. "I need [specific decision / resource] by [date] to [enable action]." If the council can't state the Ask in one sentence, the proposal isn't ready to vote on — it's still a thinking exercise.

**4. What does this do to EPC / LTGP?** (LTV Workshop — Hormozi)
For any decision touching revenue, acquisition, retention, or unit economics: what's the expected delta on EPC (Earnings Per Click) and LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit)? These are the North Star metrics. If the proposer can't answer this — even directionally — Warren Margin and Gordan Offer will force the question before voting begins.

**5. Is this THP or HTP?** (Gatena Leadership — core diagnostic)
- **THP (There-Here-Path)** — Leadership mode. Start with the destination (THERE), assess current reality honestly (HERE), build the bridge (PATH). Creative, proactive, mission-driven.
- **HTP (Here-There-Path)** — Escape mode. Start with "I don't like where I am," jump to "I want relief," reach for a quick fix. Reactive, short-term, often drift-driven.

Most bad decisions look strategic but are actually HTP in disguise — a pivot away from discomfort rather than a move toward purpose. The Council surfaces this by asking: is this decision being *pulled* by a clear THERE, or *pushed* by a painful HERE?

**6. Which leg of the Three-Legged Stool is being tested?** (Gatena Leadership — systems diagnostic)
```
          Purpose (the seat)
                 |
        Leadership (the side)
         /       |       \
     Op         Org      Fin
  Strategy   Strategy  Strategy
```
Every high-stakes decision primarily loads one of the three legs: Operational (how we create/deliver value), Organizational (how we structure/lead people), Financial (how we sustain resources). The Council asks two questions: *Which leg does this primarily load?* And: *Are the other two legs strong enough to absorb the weight?* If one leg is already fraying, loading it further destabilizes the whole stool regardless of how attractive the decision looks in isolation.

---

**Why this matters:** The Council is expensive cognitive bandwidth. A badly-framed or badly-motivated decision wastes all 9 members' time. Running the Pre-Council Diagnostic up front means the Council vote is sharper, faster, more quantitative, and more grounded in mission rather than reaction.
