# CEO Council — Voting Protocol

## Core Rules

1. **No abstentions.** Every member votes on every decision. If a member's domain isn't directly implicated, they still vote — every decision has second-order effects in every domain.
2. **Three vote options:** APPROVE | APPROVE WITH CONDITIONS | REJECT
3. **Majority wins:** 5 of 9 (or 6 of 10/11 when optional members are present)
4. **Conditions are binding.** An APPROVE WITH CONDITIONS vote is not a soft yes — the conditions must be explicitly addressed before execution proceeds.
5. **Numbers Requirement (Hormozi Standard).** Every quantitative claim cites a specific number, not a directional adjective. "Improves retention" is not allowed; "improves Day 30 retention from 42% to a target of 48%" is. Warren Margin and Gordan Offer will force this standard on every member.

## Domain Scoring

Each member scores the decision 1–10 from their domain lens before voting:
- 8–10: Strong fit / minimal concern
- 5–7: Acceptable with monitoring or conditions
- 1–4: Significant concern; likely REJECT unless conditions resolve it

The score surfaces *where* the risk lives, not just *whether* to proceed.

## The Transcendent There Check

Before voting, every member must answer one question:

> **Does this decision move us closer to or further from the 3-year vision?**

- **Closer** → adds weight toward APPROVE
- **Neutral** → no effect on vote
- **Further** → adds weight toward REJECT or CONDITIONS
- **Unclear** → member must flag this explicitly; ambiguity is a condition, not a pass

This check is non-negotiable. It ensures no short-term revenue win, no tactical urgency, and no external pressure overrides strategic coherence.

### Nesting THEREs Cascade (Gatena Leadership)

The Transcendent There check is not a binary yes/no — it's a cascade. A decision can be *Closer* at one level of the THERE and *Further* at another. Members walk through all four levels before scoring:

```
Level 1 — Transcendent There  → "Inspiring the world to pray."
Level 2 — Strategic There      → The 3-year vision (#1 faith-tech platform, Profit AND Purpose)
Level 3 — Milestone Theres     → 12-month operating goals (ARR, FTE, retention, moat depth)
Level 4 — Tactical Theres      → Quarterly SMART goals tied to each milestone
```

- A decision that advances Level 3 (the quarter) but degrades Level 1 (the mission) is **Further**, not Closer, regardless of P&L impact. Miriam Covenant will veto this every time.
- A decision that advances Level 1 (the mission) but damages Level 3 (the quarter) needs explicit conditions on how the business bridges the short-term cost. Warren Margin and Gordan Offer will score this harshly.
- A decision that is Neutral across Levels 1–2 but positive on Level 3 is a tactical choice — it should not consume Council bandwidth in the first place. Leila Execute flags it back to the proposer.

**Rule:** If a decision moves Closer on some Levels and Further on others, the member must name *which* Levels and *by how much* before voting. "Closer overall" is not an acceptable answer.

## The Rest Diagnostic (Leila Hormozi — Secondary Check)

After the Transcendent There check, every member also runs the Rest Diagnostic:

> **If the CEO disappeared for 30 days, does this decision still execute correctly?**

- **Yes** → the system is real; the decision is a system, not a dependency
- **No** → the decision is a hidden CEO dependency dressed up as a system

A "No" answer doesn't auto-fail the vote — but it must be surfaced explicitly and paired with a condition that removes the CEO from the critical path. Leila Execute is the primary enforcer; Rex Culture will second her.

## Miriam's Mission Veto (Red Line)

Miriam Covenant holds a **Mission Veto** — the only unilateral veto in the council.

She may invoke it when a decision would:
- Compromise Pray.com's covenant with its faith audience (e.g., predatory monetization of vulnerable users)
- Damage trust with the spiritual community in a way that is difficult or impossible to repair
- Directly contradict the stated mission ("inspiring the world to pray")

The Mission Veto **overrides a majority vote**. It is not used lightly — Miriam must provide explicit justification citing mission, user trust, or long-term brand damage. It can be appealed by the CEO (Steve) with a written override rationale.

## Escalation

If the vote is tied (only possible with even-number councils including optional members):
1. The member with the lowest domain score re-examines their vote with the conditions on the table
2. If still tied, the decision escalates to Steve with a full council brief — no forced consensus

- [Condition 2 — raised by Member Y]

- [The strongest REJECT argument, even if it lost]
