# D.R.E.A.M. Culture System

A values-driven culture operating system for purpose-driven organizations. The D.R.E.A.M. framework provides a concrete, measurable system for defining and scaling organizational culture.

---

## The Purpose-Driven Foundation

Every organization using this system should define three things:

- **Vision (The Why):** The legacy you want to leave — the ultimate impact
- **Mission (The How):** The daily work that moves you toward the vision
- **Product (The What):** The concrete thing you build to fulfill the mission

**North Star:** "Optimize your business for Profit & Purpose."

---

## Purpose Driven Team

### Good Flowstate vs. Bad Flowstate

**Good Flowstate (Gold Triangle):** Coach — Challenger — Creator
- Powered by: Commitment & Curiosity
- The triangle of healthy team dynamics

**Bad Flowstate (Red Triangle):** Hero — Villain — Victim
- Powered by: Adrenaline & Drama
- The triangle of toxic team dynamics

**Expectation:** "We expect everyone to lead & be led with good flowstate."

## D.R.E.A.M. Values

### D — Devotion
*"We operate from a mission, team, self perspective. We believe that faith is our most valuable asset and we devote our time towards helping others grow it."*

**Personal commitment:** "I will demonstrate radical endurance and exhibit a consistent commitment towards our mission, our team, and myself."

**Degrees of Devotion (5-level pyramid):**

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Transcendent** | "How do I help the mission?" I intentionally work to build the mission and to help others. I'm committed for the long term and value us all winning together. I share the impact. (Mission > Team > Self) |
| 4 | **Builders** | "How do I help the opportunity?" I am effective at getting my job done, responsible towards teammates, and I generally do well individually. I am a solid employee. |
| 3 | **Achievers** | "How do I keep my job?" I do my job well enough to be noticed but sometimes lack commitment. I contribute, but the team or the objective is the last thing I think about. |
| 2 | **Chameleons** | "How do I stay?" I do the bare minimum. I'm adaptable enough to blend in but I lack genuine passion about the mission, the team, or the objective. |
| 1 | **Opportunist** | "What's in it for me?" I put myself first. (Self > Team > Mission) |

### R — Research
*"We seek to quantify, test, and share our intuition. By using wisdom, insight, and facts we make better decisions more often."*

**Personal commitment:** "I will learn, share, and apply new information everyday. I will quantify my anecdotal insights and help others understand the data behind my decisions."

**Degrees of Research (5-level pyramid):**

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Innovator** | "The data points to... so I did an experiment to learn... here are the key insights... and this is what we should expect if..." |
| 4 | **Data Driven** | "Industry shows... 'Data indicates...' 'Results of the A/B test are...' 'Our customers said...'" |
| 3 | **Comparison Driver** | "'From these situations, we can infer...' 'This app did...' 'At X company, they have...'" |
| 2 | **Experience Driven** | "'In my experience...' 'At my last company...' 'I think...'" |
| 1 | **Opinion Driven** | "'I feel...' 'I believe...' 'I heard someone say...'" |

**Data Informed = Facts + Feelings.** Both matter. Feelings are explored through feelings, hunches, and instincts by doing and learning. Understanding that feelings are very important, understanding opposing views is difficult, and learning the facts helps realize impact.

### E — Empathy
*"We listen well, look to understand, and demonstrate the respect needed to add value across different groups."*

**Personal commitment:** "I will seek to understand, ask clarifying questions, and communicate concepts in 'see it, hear it, touch it' formats, while treating everyone with respect."

**Degrees of Empathy (5-level pyramid, based on Maslow):**

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Self Actualization** (External Focus + Internal Focus) | "I strive to understand others who are different than me. I seek to help people love their life. I add value to people's lives everyday." |
| 4 | **Importance** | "I am a vital part of this company. I am an important person in someone's career. I consistently make meaningful contributions to the product I work on." |
| 3 | **Belonging** | "I know that I'm part of something bigger. It's nice to work here. I could leave with a better offer but this place is a great place to grow." |
| 2 | **Security** | "I don't like my job. My boss and my team is… I don't feel like my job is at risk, but I don't feel like I'm able to contribute as much as I think I can." |
| 1 | **Survival** | "I hate the people in this company. I am here because I have to feed my family. This was the best of the available opportunities. I try not to think about it." |

---

### A — Application
*"We can be relied on to create solutions. We are people of action who work incredibly hard to master our craft and help others with their craft."*

**Personal commitment:** "I will be great in my role by delivering quality work, helping others succeed, and regularly providing candid feedback to my team so we can improve."

**Degrees of Application (5-level pyramid):**

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Resolve** (Solving the Puzzle + Ignoring the Problem) | "I identified a problem. Figured out what caused it, researched how to fix it, and I fixed it, here's how to prevent it in the future." |
| 4 | **Recommend** | "Here's a problem, here's what I think caused it, here are some recommended solutions, and here are the solutions I think we should pursue." |
| 3 | **Observe** | "Here's a problem, here's what I've noticed about it, and here are some possible solutions." |
| 2 | **Research** | "There is a problem, and I've found some potential causes." |
| 1 | **Complain** | "There is a problem. Not my problem. Not going away far enough away from it." |

---

### M — Mindset
*"We lead the market with BIG IDEAS that create powerful first, second, and third order effects for our customers. We know anything is possible."*

**Personal commitment:** "I will inspire others with big ideas, suspend judgement of conflicting beliefs, and think through long term effects from a position that anything is possible."

**Degrees of Mindset (5-level pyramid):**

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Innocent Wonderment** (Succeeding Together + Surviving Alone) | "Life is great and while there are many challenges in life, all people have infinite potential to make anything possible." |
| 4 | **Tribal Pride** | "We are great, our values are great, and our team is the best. When we work together as one, we can do anything." |
| 3 | **Lone Warrior** | "I am great. I find greatness in setting and achieving goals. I am resilient. I will continue to be an all star and will do everything in my power to succeed." |
| 2 | **Apathetic Victim** | "This place sucks and this life sucks right now. The odds of winning are against me and the system is set against us around me. No kidding." |
| 1 | **Despairing Hostility** | "Life is horrible. What happens to me is predetermined. There is nothing I can do to change it and they are doing this to me on purpose." |

## Personal Impact Funnel

A cascading alignment tool from abstract to specific:

1. **Vision** (1, Abstract) — Leave a legacy of helping others by providing every ministry with a powerful platform for growth & innovation
2. **Mission** — Help people grow their faith & cultivate their community by connecting them to ministries around the world
3. **Values** — Devotion, Research, Empathy, Application, Mindset
4. **Objectives** — Your organization's primary strategic objective
5. **Strategies** — The key initiatives that advance the objective
6. **Tactics** (Many, Specific) — The concrete actions and campaigns
