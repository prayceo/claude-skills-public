## The 12 Competencies in Detail

### Domain 1: Product Execution

#### 1. Feature Specification

**Definition:** Clear, actionable communication of requirements and goals to teams.

**Over-Performing:**
- PRDs are comprehensive yet concise -- engineers have no ambiguity
- Edge cases are anticipated and addressed before implementation
- Acceptance criteria are testable and measurable
- Requirements evolve gracefully as new information emerges
- Balances thoroughness with speed -- knows when a sketch is enough vs. when a full spec is needed

**Needs Focus:**
- Engineers frequently ask clarifying questions mid-sprint
- Edge cases are discovered during QA, requiring rework
- Requirements change frequently after implementation begins
- Specs are either too vague or too detailed (micromanaging implementation)

**Development Activities:**
- Review specs from senior PMs and note what makes them effective
- Have an engineering lead review your spec before sharing with the team
- Practice writing specs at three levels of fidelity: sketch, outline, full spec

#### 2. Product Delivery

**Definition:** Iterating rapidly with cross-functional teams toward defined objectives.

**Over-Performing:**
- Ships consistently -- hits deadlines or communicates changes early
- Proactively identifies and unblocks team dependencies
- Manages scope effectively -- can cut scope without losing the core value
- Creates a rhythm of delivery that builds team confidence and momentum
- Balances speed and quality

**Needs Focus:**
- Deadlines regularly missed without early warning
- PM is the bottleneck -- decisions pile up waiting for their input
- Scope creep is common
- Team does not have a reliable cadence of delivery

**Development Activities:**
- Track delivery velocity: features planned vs. shipped per sprint
- Practice scope cutting: for every feature, identify the 50% scope version
- Establish rituals: standups, sprint planning, retros

#### 3. Quality Assurance

**Definition:** Identifying and resolving technical, functional, and business quality issues.

**Over-Performing:**
- Catches issues before users do through systematic testing
- Has clear quality standards for different types of releases
- Balances quality bar with delivery speed appropriately
- Manages tech debt as a portfolio -- knows when to invest and when to defer
- Monitors production quality metrics and responds to regressions

**Needs Focus:**
- Users report bugs that should have been caught pre-release
- Technical debt accumulates with no plan to address it
- Quality standards are inconsistent across releases
- PM is unaware of production quality metrics

### Domain 2: Customer Insight

#### 4. Fluency with Data

**Definition:** Generating actionable insights from metrics and connecting them to business outcomes.

**Over-Performing:**
- Defines metrics that directly measure user and business value
- Identifies meaningful trends before they become obvious
- Distinguishes signal from noise
- Connects product metrics to business outcomes (engagement to revenue to LTV)
- Uses data to prioritize, not just to report

**Needs Focus:**
- Relies on intuition when data is available
- Tracks vanity metrics without connecting to business impact
- Cannot explain why a metric moved
- Overfits to data -- treats every fluctuation as meaningful

**Development Activities:**
- Weekly metric review: pick one metric, explain its trend, hypothesize the cause
- Build a metric tree connecting feature metrics to company KPIs
- Practice: "If this metric dropped 10%, what three things would you investigate?"

#### 5. Voice of the Customer

**Definition:** Leveraging user feedback across multiple channels to inform decisions.

**Over-Performing:**
- Talks to users regularly (4+ conversations per month)
- Synthesizes feedback across channels (support, reviews, interviews, data)
- Separates user requests from underlying needs
- Shares customer insights broadly across the organization
- Can articulate the user's perspective in any product discussion

**Needs Focus:**
- Rarely talks to users directly
- Relies on second-hand information
- Takes user requests at face value without probing underlying needs
- Makes product decisions without customer evidence

**Development Activities:**
- Require 4+ user conversations per month; track in 1:1s
- Create a research share ritual at weekly team meetings
- Segment rotation: each month, talk to a different user type (new, churned, power, free, paid)
- Assumption audit: before any feature decision, list assumptions and star the ones with evidence

#### 6. User Experience Design

**Definition:** Collaborating on interaction design that makes customer needs easy to accomplish.

**Over-Performing:**
- Has strong design taste -- can identify what makes an experience good or bad
- Provides specific, constructive feedback in design reviews
- Advocates for user needs when design trades off with technical constraints
- Understands UX principles (information hierarchy, cognitive load, affordances)
- Collaborates with designers as a thought partner, not just an approver

### Domain 3: Product Strategy

#### 7. Business Outcome Ownership

**Definition:** Driving meaningful business results by connecting product goals to strategic objectives.

This is the most critical competency. It remains equally important at every seniority level -- from APM to CPO. A junior PM owns metrics for their feature. A CPO owns metrics for the company. The scale changes; the mindset does not.

**Over-Performing:**
- Owns a metric, not a feature backlog
- Can quantify the expected business impact of every roadmap item
- Takes accountability for outcomes, not just outputs
- Adjusts course when a shipped feature does not move the metric
- Thinks in terms of ROI -- where should the team invest for maximum return?

**Needs Focus:**
- Measures success by features shipped, not outcomes achieved
- Cannot articulate how their work connects to company revenue or growth
- Celebrates launches without measuring impact
- Does not adjust strategy based on post-launch results

**Development Activities:**
- Assign metric ownership, not feature ownership
- Require weekly metric reviews with root cause analysis
- Impact prediction: before any feature ships, predict expected metric impact; after ship, compare
- 30-day post-mortem discipline for every shipped feature

#### 8. Product Vision & Roadmapping

**Definition:** Defining compelling visions that ladder features into coherent product direction.

**Over-Performing:**
- Roadmap tells a story -- each initiative connects to a clear narrative
- Balances short-term wins with long-term bets
- Inspires the team with a compelling vision
- Roadmap is a living document that adapts to new information
- Can defend every item's inclusion and explain what was intentionally excluded

**Development Activities:**
- Write the press release for the next feature before building it
- Practice articulating the "why" behind every roadmap item
- Challenge exercise: "If we could only ship one thing next quarter, what and why?"

#### 9. Strategic Impact

**Definition:** Contributing to business strategy and using products as levers for company goals.

**Over-Performing:**
- Thinks beyond their product area to company-level strategy
- Identifies strategic opportunities before leadership asks
- Can articulate competitive dynamics and market positioning
- Shapes company direction through product insights

### Domain 4: Influencing People

#### 10. Stakeholder Management

**Definition:** Proactively engaging impacted parties and rallying support for decisions.

**Over-Performing:**
- No surprises -- stakeholders are informed before decisions are announced
- Stakeholders feel heard even when their requests are deprioritized
- Builds coalition before big decisions -- arrives at the meeting with alignment
- Manages expectations proactively when timelines or scope change
- Different stakeholders receive appropriately tailored communication

#### 11. Team Leadership

**Definition:** Coaching and mentoring direct reports with autonomy and clear expectations.

**Over-Performing:**
- Team members visibly grow under their leadership
- Delegates effectively -- empowers without abandoning
- Gives actionable, specific feedback frequently
- Builds team culture that attracts talent
- Holds the team to high standards while providing support

#### 12. Managing Up

**Definition:** Achieving alignment with leadership to secure resources and strategic support.

**Over-Performing:**
- Leadership trusts their judgment and delegates strategic decisions
- Gets resources approved because proposals are well-framed and data-backed
- Frames decisions in terms leadership cares about (revenue, growth, risk)
- Proactively shares updates before leadership has to ask
- Can influence leadership's thinking on strategic direction

## Competency Evolution by Seniority

```
JUNIOR PM (APM/PM)
  Primary focus: Product Execution
  Secondary: Customer Insight
  Emerging: Product Strategy, Influencing People
  Key transition: Moving from "told what to build" to "figures out what to build"

MID-LEVEL PM (Senior PM)
  Primary focus: Customer Insight + Product Strategy
  Secondary: Product Execution (mastered), Influencing People
  Key transition: Moving from feature ownership to outcome ownership

SENIOR PM (Group PM / Director)
  Primary focus: Product Strategy + Influencing People
  Secondary: Customer Insight
  Key transition: Moving from building products to building teams that build products

EXECUTIVE (VP / CPO)
  Primary focus: Influencing People + Strategic Impact
  Secondary: Product Strategy
  Key transition: Moving from team leadership to organizational design
```

## The Four PM Archetypes

### Shape 1: The Product Executor

**Strengths:** Feature Specification, Product Delivery, Quality Assurance
**Natural tendency:** Ship fast, ship well, manage complexity
**Growth edge:** Can build the wrong thing perfectly. Needs stronger Customer Insight and Product Strategy.
**Best deployed on:** Complex technical products, platform features, infrastructure, scaling challenges

### Shape 2: The Customer Champion

**Strengths:** Voice of the Customer, User Experience Design, Fluency with Data
**Natural tendency:** Deep empathy, user-centric design, data-informed decisions
**Growth edge:** May over-index on user needs at the expense of business outcomes. Needs stronger Strategic Impact and Business Outcome Ownership.
**Best deployed on:** Core user experience, onboarding, retention-focused features

### Shape 3: The Strategic Visionary

**Strengths:** Product Vision & Roadmapping, Strategic Impact, Business Outcome Ownership
**Natural tendency:** Big-picture thinking, connecting product to business, inspiring direction
**Growth edge:** May struggle with execution details and cross-functional collaboration. Needs stronger Product Delivery and Stakeholder Management.
**Best deployed on:** New product areas, 0-to-1 initiatives, strategic pivots

### Shape 4: The People Leader

**Strengths:** Stakeholder Management, Team Leadership, Managing Up
**Natural tendency:** Build alignment, develop talent, navigate organizational complexity
**Growth edge:** May default to managing process rather than driving product outcomes. Needs stronger Customer Insight and Product Execution.
**Best deployed on:** Cross-functional programs, organizational transformations, large team leadership

### Team-Level Calibration

After evaluating each PM individually, plot the team on a heat map:

```
                    Over-Perf   On Track   Needs Focus
Feature Spec         PM1         PM2,PM3
Product Delivery     PM2         PM1        PM3
Quality Assurance                PM1,PM2    PM3
Data Fluency         PM3         PM1        PM2
Voice of Customer               PM2,PM3    PM1
UX Design            PM1         PM3        PM2
Business Outcomes               PM1        PM2,PM3
Vision/Roadmap       PM2         PM1,PM3
Strategic Impact                PM2        PM1,PM3
Stakeholder Mgmt     PM1         PM2,PM3
Team Leadership                 PM1        PM2,PM3
Managing Up                     PM1,PM2    PM3
```

**Reading the heat map:**
- Columns with many entries: team-level strength or weakness
- Rows with spread: healthy -- different PMs bring different strengths
- "Needs Focus" clusters: indicate hiring priorities or team-wide training needs

### Interview Focus by Archetype Needed

**Hiring a Product Executor:**
- Interview for: Feature Specification, Product Delivery, Quality Assurance
- Test: Case study with ambiguous requirements and tight timeline

**Hiring a Customer Champion:**
- Interview for: Voice of Customer, Data Fluency, UX Design
- Test: Present conflicting user feedback; ask for synthesis and recommendation

**Hiring a Strategic Visionary:**
- Interview for: Business Outcomes, Vision, Strategic Impact
- Test: Create a product strategy for a hypothetical 12-month period

**Hiring a People Leader:**
- Interview for: Stakeholder Management, Team Leadership, Managing Up
- Test: Role-play a difficult stakeholder conversation
