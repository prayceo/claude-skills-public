## Overview

This reference provides the step-by-step execution guide for building and running an SEO strategy. It covers the strategic planning phase (where to invest), the execution phase (how to create and optimize content), and the measurement phase (how to prove ROI and maintain stakeholder support).

The guide integrates Ethan Smith's core principles: the 5% focus on high-impact work, topics over keywords, content enhancement before creation, and SERP analysis as the foundation of content decisions.

## Phase 1: Strategic Assessment

### Prerequisites for SEO Investment

Before investing in SEO, confirm three conditions:

1. **Product-market fit exists.** SEO amplifies demand that already exists. It cannot create demand for a product no one wants. If users are not retaining or paying, fix the product before investing in SEO.

2. **Unit economics are sustainable.** SEO requires 6-12 months before producing meaningful results. The business must be able to sustain this investment without seeing immediate returns. If the company needs revenue next month, SEO is the wrong channel.

3. **The business model is search-compatible.** Can the value proposition be captured in search-discoverable content? Products that solve problems people actively search for are SEO-compatible. Products that create novel categories may need to build search demand first through other channels.

### Competitive Landscape Analysis

Before building strategy, understand the competitive context:

**Step 1: Identify SEO competitors**
- These are the domains that rank for your target topics — not necessarily your product competitors
- Search your top 10 target queries and note which domains appear consistently
- A news site or Wikipedia may be your SEO competitor even though they sell nothing

**Step 2: Assess competitive difficulty**
- Use domain authority scores (Ahrefs DR, Moz DA) as a rough proxy
- More importantly: analyze the content quality of ranking pages
- If top-ranking pages are thin or outdated, the opportunity is larger regardless of domain authority
- If top-ranking pages are comprehensive and frequently updated, the barrier is higher

**Step 3: Find competitive gaps**
- What topics do your competitors cover poorly or not at all?
- What topics are growing in search volume that competitors have not addressed?
- Where do you have a unique perspective, data, or expertise that competitors lack?

### Authority Domain Identification

**Step 1: List your genuine expertise areas**
- What topics does your brand have credibility on?
- What topics do your users trust you to speak about?
- What unique data, research, or experience do you have?

**Step 2: Validate with search data**
- Is there search volume for these topics?
- Are people actively searching for answers in your expertise areas?
- What is the commercial intent behind these searches? (informational vs. transactional)

**Step 3: Map the opportunity**
- For each authority domain, estimate: total search volume, competitive difficulty, commercial value
- Prioritize authority domains that combine: high search volume + lower competitive difficulty + high commercial relevance

## Phase 2: Content Strategy

### Topics Over Keywords

Smith's approach replaces traditional keyword-level SEO with topic-level strategy.

**Traditional keyword approach (outdated):**
1. Find a keyword with volume
2. Write a page targeting that keyword
3. Hope it ranks
4. Repeat

**Topic authority approach (current):**
1. Identify a topic cluster
2. Map all sub-topics and related queries within the cluster
3. Create a comprehensive pillar page covering the topic broadly
4. Create focused supporting pages for each sub-topic
5. Link everything together
6. Build authority over the entire topic simultaneously

**Why the topic approach works better:**
- Search engines evaluate expertise at the topic level, not the keyword level
- A comprehensive topic cluster sends stronger authority signals than isolated keyword pages
- A single well-structured page can rank for hundreds of related keywords
- Internal links between cluster pages strengthen the entire cluster
- Users prefer comprehensive, interconnected content

### Building a Topic Cluster

**Step 1: Choose the pillar topic**
- This should be a broad topic that covers an entire area of your expertise
- Example for your product: "Daily Engagement Practice"

**Step 2: Identify sub-topics**
- Use keyword research tools to find all queries related to the pillar
- Check "People Also Ask" in Google for related questions
- Review competitor content for topics they cover
- Ask your customer support team what questions users ask
- Example sub-topics: "morning daily routine," "how to engage for 30 minutes," "personal journaling," "content for anxiety," "intercessory engagement guide"

**Step 3: Create the pillar page**
- 2,000-5,000 words covering the broad topic comprehensively
- Organized with clear headings for each major sub-topic
- Links to all supporting pages for deeper dives
- Updated regularly (at least quarterly)

**Step 4: Create supporting pages**
- One page per sub-topic, 1,000-2,000 words
- More focused and specific than the pillar
- Links back to the pillar and to related supporting pages
- Answers a specific question or addresses a specific need

**Step 5: Build internal links**
- Pillar links to all supporting pages
- Each supporting page links back to the pillar
- Supporting pages link to 2-3 related supporting pages
- Use descriptive, varied anchor text

## Phase 3: Content Execution

### Content Enhancement (Priority 1)

Before creating any new content, enhance existing content. This is almost always the highest-ROI SEO activity.

**Enhancement process:**

1. **Identify enhancement candidates**
   - Open Google Search Console → Performance → Pages
   - Filter for pages ranking in positions 5-20 (page 2 or bottom of page 1)
   - Sort by impressions (these pages are getting seen but not clicked)

2. **Analyze query coverage**
   - For each candidate page, check the Queries tab
   - Look for queries the page ranks for but does not fully address in the content
   - These are your enhancement opportunities

3. **Enhance the content**
   - Add sections addressing the uncovered queries
   - Add the relevant terms to the title and headings (if they fit naturally)
   - Expand thin sections with more detail, examples, and practical advice
   - Update any outdated information
   - Add internal links to and from the page

4. **Republish and monitor**
   - Update the publication date (signals freshness to search engines)
   - Monitor rank changes over 2-4 weeks
   - Track traffic changes over 4-8 weeks

**Expected results:** Pages ranking on page 2 frequently jump to page 1 within 2-4 weeks after meaningful enhancement. Pages in positions 5-10 may move into the top 3-5.

### New Content Creation (Priority 2)

After enhancement opportunities are exhausted, create new content.

**Content brief template:**

```
Target Topic: [Main topic this page will cover]
Target Queries: [3-5 specific queries this page should rank for]
Content Format: [Listicle / How-to / Guide / Comparison / Reference]
  (Based on SERP analysis — what format currently ranks?)
Word Count Target: [Based on SERP analysis — how long are ranking pages?]
Pillar or Supporting: [Is this a pillar page or supporting content?]
Internal Link Targets: [Which existing pages should link to/from this page?]

SERP Analysis:
- Top 3 ranking pages: [URLs]
- Common sections across top pages: [List]
- Sections missing from top pages (our opportunity): [List]
- Content format: [What type of page ranks?]
- Average word count: [Number]

Outline:
[H1: Title]
[H2: Section 1]
  [Key points to cover]
[H2: Section 2]
  [Key points to cover]
...

Unique Value:
[What will our page offer that existing ranking pages do not?]
[Unique data, examples, perspective, or comprehensiveness]
```

### SERP Analysis: Matching Content to Intent

Before creating any page, analyze the search results for your target queries.

**SERP analysis checklist:**

1. **Search the target query in Google** (ideally in incognito/private mode)

2. **Identify the dominant content type:**
   - Informational (blog posts, guides, Wikipedia)
   - Commercial (product pages, comparison pages, reviews)
   - Navigational (brand pages, login pages)
   - Transactional (purchase pages, pricing pages)

3. **Identify the dominant format:**
   - Listicle ("10 Best...")
   - How-to guide ("How to...")
   - In-depth guide ("Complete Guide to...")
   - Comparison ("X vs. Y")
   - Product/landing page
   - Video results

4. **Analyze content depth:**
   - Average word count of top 5 results
   - Common sections and headings
   - Supporting media (images, videos, tables, infographics)

5. **Match your content:**
   - If the SERP shows listicles, create a listicle
   - If the SERP shows 3,000-word guides, create a comprehensive guide
   - Do NOT fight the SERP — match the format that Google has determined matches user intent

## Phase 4: Internal Linking Strategy

### Why Internal Linking Is High Impact

Internal links serve three critical functions:

1. **Discovery:** Search engine crawlers follow internal links to find new and updated content. Pages with no incoming internal links may never be discovered.

2. **Authority distribution:** Link equity flows from page to page through internal links. High-authority pages can boost lower-authority pages by linking to them.

3. **Topical signals:** Internal links with descriptive anchor text tell search engines what the linked page is about, reinforcing topical relevance.

### The 7+ Link Rule

Smith's guideline: every important page should have 7 or more internal links pointing to it from other pages on the site. Pages with fewer than 7 internal links are likely underperforming their ranking potential.

### Internal Linking Audit Process

1. **Crawl the site** using Screaming Frog, Sitebulb, or Ahrefs Site Audit
2. **Export internal link data** — page URL, number of incoming internal links
3. **Sort by importance** — identify high-value pages (pages targeting important topics, product pages, conversion pages)
4. **Flag under-linked pages** — high-value pages with fewer than 7 incoming internal links
5. **Build a fix plan** — for each under-linked page, identify 5-10 existing pages where a contextual link would be natural

### Types of Internal Links (by Impact)

| Link Type | Impact | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Body content contextual links | High | Links within article body text, surrounded by relevant context |
| Related content sections | Medium-High | "Related articles" or "Further reading" sections at the bottom of pages |
| Navigation menu links | Medium | Main navigation, sidebar navigation |
| Footer links | Low | Site-wide footer links (diluted by appearing on every page) |
| Breadcrumb links | Medium | Hierarchical breadcrumb navigation |
| Sidebar links | Low-Medium | Sidebar recommendations or category lists |

**Priority order:** Always prioritize body content contextual links over navigational links. Search engines weight contextual links (surrounded by relevant text) higher than navigational links.

### Anchor Text Best Practices

- Use descriptive anchor text that describes what the linked page is about
- Vary anchor text — do not use the exact same phrase for every link to a page
- Avoid generic anchors ("click here," "read more," "learn more")
- Do not over-optimize — anchor text should read naturally in context

## Phase 5: Measurement and ROI

### Core SEO Metrics

**Organic traffic:**
- Total organic sessions (Google Analytics or equivalent)
- Organic sessions by page and by topic cluster
- Organic new users vs. returning users
- Organic session trends (week over week, month over month)

**Search performance:**
- Impressions (Google Search Console)
- Clicks (Google Search Console)
- Average position by query and page
- Click-through rate by position and page

**Business impact:**
- Organic-sourced conversions (app installs, subscriptions, sign-ups)
- Revenue attributed to organic traffic
- Organic LTV vs. paid LTV
- Cost per organic acquisition (SEO investment / organic conversions)

### SEO ROI Calculation

**Monthly ROI framework:**

```
Organic Revenue Value = Organic Conversions x Average Revenue per Conversion

Equivalent Paid Cost = Organic Clicks x Average CPC for Those Queries

SEO Investment = Team cost + Tool cost + Content cost

ROI = (Organic Revenue Value - SEO Investment) / SEO Investment

Equivalent Paid Cost ROI = Equivalent Paid Cost / SEO Investment
```

**Example for your product:**
- 10,000 organic clicks per month
- Average CPC for engagement/product queries: $1.50
- Equivalent paid cost: $15,000/month
- SEO investment: $8,000/month (content team + tools)
- Equivalent paid cost ROI: $15,000 / $8,000 = 1.9x

The compounding nature of SEO makes this more compelling over time. Unlike paid, where spending stops and traffic stops, SEO traffic continues and grows even during periods of reduced investment.

### Executive Reporting Framework

**Monthly report structure:**
1. Headline metrics: organic traffic, organic conversions, organic revenue
2. Top-performing content: which pages drove the most traffic and conversions
3. Ranking progress: movement on target keywords/topics
4. Content velocity: pages enhanced, pages created, pages planned
5. ROI calculation: organic value vs. investment
6. Next month priorities: what the team will focus on

**Quarterly strategy review:**
1. Portfolio performance: how are the topic clusters performing?
2. Competitive shifts: have competitors changed their SEO approach?
3. Opportunity analysis: what new topics or formats should we explore?
4. Resource request: what the team needs for the next quarter

## your product SEO Execution Roadmap

### Quarter 1: Foundation and Quick Wins

**Month 1:**
- Technical audit: rendering check, internal linking audit, crawl efficiency review
- Google Search Console setup and baseline measurement
- Competitive landscape analysis for engagement session topics
- Identify top 20 content enhancement opportunities

**Month 2:**
- Execute content enhancement on top 10 pages
- Create editorial calendar for 3 topic clusters
- Begin pillar page creation for "Daily Engagement Guide"
- Fix any technical issues identified in Month 1

**Month 3:**
- Complete first pillar page with 5 supporting articles
- Enhance remaining 10 pages from Month 1 audit
- Begin second topic cluster ("Content for Specific Situations")
- Manually create 10 programmatic topic pages (test template)
- Report Q1 results and set Q2 targets

### Quarter 2: Scale and AEO

**Month 4:**
- Launch AEO strategy: 10 question-based landing pages
- Continue editorial content production (4-6 articles/month)
- Evaluate programmatic page performance, scale if positive
- Begin YouTube content video series

**Month 5:**
- Scale programmatic pages to 50+ if Q1 test was positive
- Continue AEO landing page creation
- Begin Reddit participation in consumer communities
- Optimize help center content for AEO

**Month 6:**
- Q2 performance review and strategy adjustment
- Launch third topic cluster
- Full AEO measurement baseline established
- ROI presentation to stakeholders

### Ongoing: Maintenance and Optimization

- Monthly content enhancement cycle (refresh existing content every 6-12 months)
- Weekly rank tracking and anomaly detection
- Monthly competitive monitoring
- Quarterly topic cluster performance review
- Biannual strategy review and rebalancing

## SEO Measurement: Metrics and Reporting Cadence

### Traffic Metrics
- Organic sessions (total and by page)
- Organic click-through rate (from search results)
- Impressions and average position (Google Search Console)
- Pages indexed vs. pages submitted

### Business Metrics
- Organic-sourced conversions (downloads, subscriptions)
- Revenue attributed to organic traffic
- Cost-per-acquisition from organic vs. paid channels
- Organic traffic share of total traffic

### Content Metrics
- Ranking positions for target keywords/topics
- Content engagement (time on page, scroll depth, bounce rate)
- Internal link clicks (measures content navigation quality)
- Content freshness (when was content last updated?)

### AEO Metrics
- AI referral traffic (from ChatGPT, Perplexity, etc.)
- Brand mentions in AI answers (tracked via manual queries or monitoring tools)
- AEO conversion rate vs. organic search conversion rate
- Question coverage (what % of user questions does your content answer?)

### Reporting Cadence
- **Weekly:** Rank tracking, traffic anomalies, indexation issues
- **Monthly:** Full traffic and conversion report, content performance review, competitive position
- **Quarterly:** Strategy review, portfolio rebalancing, ROI calculation, stakeholder presentation
