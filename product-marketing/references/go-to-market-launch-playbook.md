# Go-to-Market Launch Playbook

**Source framework:** Mary Sheehan's launch tiering system from *The Pocket Guide to Product Launches* and the product marketing framework, supplemented by Meghan Keaney Anderson's GTM frameworks from HubSpot, Jasper, and Watershed.

---

## The GTM Maturity Model

**Level 1: Reactive GTM**
- Features ship when engineering finishes them
- Marketing learns about features days before launch
- No launch planning or tiering; success measurement is an afterthought

**Level 2: Planned GTM**
- Product and marketing align on a launch calendar
- Basic launch planning exists (email + blog post)
- Some success metrics defined; limited cross-functional coordination

**Level 3: Strategic GTM**
- Full tiering system in place with launch briefs as standard practice
- Cross-functional launch teams with clear roles
- Success metrics defined before launch, tracked after; retrospectives feed back into roadmap

**Level 4: Optimized GTM**
- Data-driven launch decisions (tier selection based on data)
- Automated launch sequences for Tier 3 launches
- Continuous messaging optimization; GTM strategy evolves based on win/loss analysis

**Your product should target Level 3** as the immediate goal, with aspiration toward Level 4.

## Launch Tiering Detail

### Tier 1: Major Launch ("Gold")

**Criteria:** New product, major pivot, feature that fundamentally changes value proposition, entry into new market, expected >10% revenue impact.

**Activities checklist:**

| Timeline | Activity | Owner |
|----------|----------|-------|
| T-12 weeks | Define launch strategy and brief | PMM |
| T-10 weeks | Align on positioning and messaging | PMM + Product |
| T-8 weeks | Creative brief and asset development begins | PMM + Creative |
| T-6 weeks | Press/media outreach begins (under embargo) | PR/Comms |
| T-4 weeks | Enable customer-facing teams (CS, community) | PMM |
| T-3 weeks | Update App Store listing (if applicable) | PMM + ASO |
| T-2 weeks | Finalize all assets and test in staging | PMM + Engineering |
| T-1 week | Final review and rehearsal | All |
| Launch day | Execute launch sequence | All |
| T+1 week | First metrics check and customer feedback review | PMM |
| T+4 weeks | Full launch retrospective | PMM |

### Tier 2: Significant Launch ("Silver")

**Criteria:** Meaningful enhancement, new content format, feature improving retention or conversion >5%, major integration.

| Timeline | Activity | Owner |
|----------|----------|-------|
| T-6 weeks | Define launch tier and brief | PMM |
| T-4 weeks | Develop messaging and key content | PMM |
| T-3 weeks | Create launch assets (email, blog, in-app, social) | PMM + Creative |
| T-2 weeks | Brief CS and community teams | PMM |
| T-1 week | Update App Store if relevant; prepare push sequence | PMM + Growth |
| Launch day | Send email, publish blog, activate in-app messaging | PMM + Growth |
| T+1 week | Review adoption metrics and feedback | PMM |
| T+2 weeks | Brief retrospective | PMM |

### Tier 3: Minor Launch ("Bronze")

**Criteria:** Incremental improvement, bug fixes, minor content additions, UX refinements.

| Timeline | Activity | Owner |
|----------|----------|-------|
| T-2 weeks | Note feature in upcoming release notes | PMM or Product |
| T-1 week | Write in-app changelog copy | PMM |
| Launch day | Update changelog, optional push notification | PMM |
| T+1 week | Monitor for any support issues | CS |

## The Launch Brief Template

```
LAUNCH BRIEF: [Launch Name]
Date: [Draft date]
Author: [PMM name]
Status: [Draft / In Review / Approved]

1. OVERVIEW
   Launch Name / Tier / Ship Date / Launch Date

2. BUSINESS CONTEXT
   Why are we building this? What metric will this impact?

3. TARGET AUDIENCE
   Primary / Secondary / Audience size estimate

4. POSITIONING
   [For (audience) who (need), this feature (benefit) unlike (alternatives)]

5. KEY MESSAGES (in priority order)
   Message 1 / Message 2 / Message 3

6. SUCCESS METRICS
   Primary / Secondary / Guardrail

7. CHANNELS AND ASSETS
   [Table: Channel | Asset | Owner | Deadline]

8. ENABLEMENT
   CS brief date, key talking points, known limitations

9. TIMELINE
   [Key milestones with dates]

10. RISKS AND MITIGATION

11. APPROVALS
    Product lead / Marketing lead / Engineering lead
```

## Launch Execution Sequence

**Day -7 to -1: Pre-launch**
- Tease the launch to existing users (in-app teaser, email preview)
- Share with press under embargo (Tier 1 only)
- Final QA of all assets and integrations
- Confirm all teams are briefed and ready

**Day 0: Launch day**
- Hour 1: Confirm feature is live and stable; activate monitoring
- Hour 2-3: Send launch email, activate in-app messaging, send push, publish blog
- Hour 4-6: Lift press embargo (Tier 1), post on social, share with partners, update App Store
- Hours 6-24: Monitor app store reviews, support channels, real-time adoption metrics

**Day 1-7: Post-launch**
- Daily metrics check; address reported issues immediately; share early results

**Day 8-30: Post-launch optimization**
- Review full 30-day metrics against goals
- Gather structured customer feedback
- Decide whether to amplify or iterate

## GTM Measurement Framework

**Tier 1 Metrics:**

| Metric | When to Measure |
|--------|----------------|
| Awareness reach (total impressions) | Week 1 |
| Feature adoption rate | Day 7, Day 30 |
| Activation rate | Day 7, Day 30 |
| Retention impact (adopters vs. non-adopters) | Day 30, Day 60 |
| Revenue impact | Day 30, Day 90 |
| NPS/satisfaction | Day 30 |

**Tier 2 Metrics:** Feature adoption rate, engagement depth, retention impact, support volume.

**Tier 3 Metrics:** Completion rate, support volume, optional adoption tracking.
