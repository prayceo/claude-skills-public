1.	Begin by gathering the current interest rate environment’s detailed data. Identify the prevailing rates for short-term and long-term borrowings, and note any anticipated trends from central banks or financial institutions that could influence these rates in the near future.

4.	Delve into the terms of the company’s debt agreements and evaluate any provisions or clauses that may be activated or become significant under various interest rate scenarios, such as rate caps, floors, or swap agreements.

10.	Ensure to communicate the potential impacts clearly and with sufficient granularity so the company can grasp the urgency and importance of each aspect of your findings.
