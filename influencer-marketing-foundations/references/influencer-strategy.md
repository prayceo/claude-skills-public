*Source: Sandy Diao, Influencer Marketing Foundations course, 2024*

## The Influencer Marketing Landscape

### Why Influencer Marketing Matters Now

Traditional paid channels (Facebook, Google, TikTok ads) face rising costs, increasing privacy restrictions, and declining effectiveness. Influencer marketing offers an alternative growth channel that leverages human trust and authenticity.

**Key advantages over paid social:**
- Authentic endorsement > brand advertisement
- Content feels native to the platform
- Can reach audiences resistant to traditional ads
- Creator content can be repurposed across channels
- Trust transfers from creator to brand

## Building an Influencer Strategy

### Step 1: Define Your Influencer Marketing Goals

**Awareness:** Expose your brand to new audiences
- Metric: reach, impressions, brand lift
- Best for: new products, category creation

**Acquisition:** Drive signups, installs, or purchases
- Metric: CPI (cost per install), CPA (cost per acquisition), attributed signups
- Best for: products with clear conversion funnels

**Engagement:** Drive deeper usage among existing users
- Metric: feature adoption, session depth, content consumption
- Best for: products seeking retention improvement

**Content:** Generate authentic content for use across channels
- Metric: content pieces produced, content performance when repurposed
- Best for: brands needing social proof and user-generated content

### Step 2: Identify Your Influencer Tiers

| Tier | Followers | Engagement Rate | Cost | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------------|------|----------|
| Nano | 1K-10K | 5-10% | $50-500 | Authenticity, niche audiences |
| Micro | 10K-100K | 3-7% | $500-5K | Targeted reach, high engagement |
| Mid | 100K-500K | 2-5% | $5K-25K | Scale + engagement balance |
| Macro | 500K-1M | 1-3% | $25K-100K | Mass awareness |
| Mega | 1M+ | 0.5-2% | $100K+ | Maximum reach, celebrity effect |

**Key insight:** Engagement rate typically decreases as follower count increases. Nano and micro influencers often deliver better ROI for acquisition goals.

### Step 3: Find the Right Influencers

**Criteria beyond follower count:**
1. **Audience alignment:** Does their audience match your target user?
2. **Content quality:** Is their content authentic and well-produced?
3. **Engagement quality:** Are comments genuine or bot-generated?
4. **Brand safety:** Is their content and behavior aligned with your brand values?
5. **Previous partnerships:** Have they worked with competitors? How did it perform?
6. **Platform fit:** Are they strong on the platform where your audience lives?

**For your product specifically:**
- Wellness-based content creators (influencers, experts with social presence)
- Wellness and mindfulness creators
- Lifestyle creators whose audience skews toward your product's demographic
- Book/reading creators who review engagement session content
- Family/parenting creators (engagement as family habit)

### Step 4: Structure the Partnership

**Partnership types:**
- **One-off sponsored post:** Single content piece, lowest commitment
- **Ambassador program:** Ongoing relationship, multiple posts, deeper integration
- **Affiliate/performance:** Creator earns commission on conversions
- **Content licensing:** Brand licenses creator content for ads/marketing
- **Co-creation:** Creator helps develop product features or content

## Campaign Execution Framework

### Brief Development

Every influencer campaign needs a clear brief:

```
Campaign: [Name]
Objective: [Awareness / Acquisition / Engagement / Content]
Key message: [1-2 sentences — what should the audience take away?]
Must-include: [Product name, key feature, CTA, tracking link/code]
Must-avoid: [Competitor mentions, claims we can't make, off-brand language]
Creative freedom: [What can the creator decide? Format, setting, personal story?]
Timeline: [Draft due → Review → Post date → Reporting]
Deliverables: [Number of posts, platforms, formats]
Compensation: [Flat fee / Performance / Hybrid]
```

**Critical rule:** Give creators creative freedom within guardrails. Over-scripted content performs worse because it doesn't feel authentic.

### Measurement Framework

**Top-of-funnel metrics:**
- Reach and impressions
- Video views and completion rates
- Brand mention volume

**Mid-funnel metrics:**
- Link clicks and click-through rate
- Profile visits and follows
- Content saves and shares

**Bottom-funnel metrics:**
- App installs (attributed via tracking link/promo code)
- Signups and trial starts
- Subscription conversions
- Cost per acquisition (CPA)
- Return on influencer spend (ROIS)

**Attribution approaches:**
1. **Unique promo codes:** Each creator gets a code; track redemptions
2. **UTM-tagged links:** Track clicks and conversions per creator
3. **Branded landing pages:** Custom landing page per creator
4. **Post-install surveys:** "How did you hear about us?" (directional, not precise)
5. **Lift analysis:** Compare install/signup rates during campaign vs. baseline

## Scaling Influencer Marketing

### From Manual to Systematic

**Phase 1: Test (1-5 influencers)**
- Test different tiers, platforms, and content styles
- Learn what resonates with your audience
- Establish baseline CPA and engagement rates

**Phase 2: Optimize (5-20 influencers)**
- Double down on what works (tier, platform, message)
- Build repeatable processes for outreach, briefing, measurement
- Create a creator relationship management system

**Phase 3: Scale (20-100+ influencers)**
- Build or buy influencer management tools
- Create ambassador programs for ongoing relationships
- Systematize content repurposing (creator content → paid ads)
- Hire dedicated influencer marketing manager

### Content Repurposing

One of the highest-ROI strategies: take creator content and use it as paid ad creative.

**Why it works:**
- Creator content looks native to the platform (less "ad-like")
- Already proven to resonate with the target audience
- Production costs are lower than traditional ad creative
- Can be tested at scale across different audiences

**Process:**
1. Negotiate content usage rights in original partnership agreement
2. Identify highest-performing organic creator content
3. Run as paid ads (whitelisted through creator's account or branded)
4. Test creator content vs. brand-produced content in A/B tests

## your product Influencer Strategy

### Ideal Creator Profiles

**Tier 1: Wellness leaders with social presence**
- Experts, experts, counselors who create engagement session content
- Audience: committed community seeking digital personal tools
- Content: "How I use your product in my daily quiet time"

**Tier 2: Wellness/mindfulness creators**
- Meditation, gratitude, and personal wellness content
- Audience: personally curious, may not identify as traditionally religious
- Content: "Adding engagement to my morning routine with your product"

**Tier 3: Family/parenting creators**
- Content about family engagement sessions, teaching kids healthy habits
- Audience: parents seeking consumer family activities
- Content: "Our family session time just got better"

### Recommended Approach
Start with 5-10 micro influencers (10K-100K followers) in the wellness and product space. Focus on acquisition (app installs) with unique promo codes. Measure CPA. If CPA is competitive with paid channels, scale to 20-50 creators within 3 months.

## Influencer Marketing Models

**Model 1: One-off sponsored posts**
- Single post or series of posts with fixed fee per post
- Best for: Testing new creators, seasonal campaigns
- Limitation: No relationship depth, inconsistent

**Model 2: Ambassador/partnership program**
- Ongoing relationship (3-12 month contract) with regular content cadence
- May include exclusivity, events, product input
- Best for: Core creators who genuinely love the product

**Model 3: Affiliate/performance-based**
- Creator gets a commission or bonus per conversion, often combined with base fee
- Requires tracking links or codes
- Best for: Aligning incentives, measuring ROI

**Model 4: UGC production (non-posting)**
- Hire creators to produce content but don't require them to post
- Use content in your own paid and organic channels
- Best for: Getting high-quality UGC-style creative for ads

**Model 5: Whitelisted/spark ads**
- Run paid ads through the creator's account
- Combines organic creator credibility with paid reach
- Available on TikTok (Spark Ads) and Meta (Branded Content Ads)
- Best for: Scaling high-performing creator content

### Anti-patterns
- Treating influencer marketing like traditional advertising (rigid scripts, no authenticity)
- Only measuring by impressions/reach (ignoring conversion and brand metrics)
- Working with creators who don't actually use or care about your product
- Not repurposing influencer content across other channels

## Measurement and Scaling

### The Influencer Measurement Stack

**Level 1: Content Metrics (per post)** — Impressions/reach, engagement rate, video views/completion, CTR
**Level 2: Conversion Metrics (per campaign)** — Installs/sign-ups attributed, CPA by creator, conversion rate, revenue attributed
**Level 3: Program Metrics (quarterly/annual)** — Total influencer-driven revenue, blended CPA vs. other channels, content production value, brand lift

### Attribution Challenges and Mitigation

Influencer marketing is hard to attribute due to delayed effects, cross-platform viewing, and word-of-mouth. Mitigation: unique discount codes per creator, UTM tracking links, branded search volume monitoring during campaigns, "How did you hear about us?" surveys, and geo-lift/conversion lift studies.

### Calculating Influencer ROI

**Direct ROI:** Revenue from tracked conversions / Total influencer spend
**Full ROI:** (Direct conversions + Estimated halo conversions + Content value) / Total spend
- Halo conversions = Branded search lift x conversion rate during campaign
- Content value = # of creative assets x cost to produce in-house equivalent

### Scaling Stages

**Stage 1: Test (Month 1-3)** — 5-10 micro influencers, test formats/messaging/platforms, $5K-$20K/month
**Stage 2: Optimize (Month 4-6)** — Double down on top performers, expand similar profiles, begin whitelisting, $20K-$50K/month
**Stage 3: Scale (Month 7-12)** — 25-50+ active creators, ambassador program, dedicated manager, $50K-$200K/month
**Stage 4: Mature (Year 2+)** — Core acquisition channel (10-30% of budget), self-serve platform, data-driven selection, $200K+/month

### Scaling Anti-patterns
- Scaling spend before establishing ROI at small scale
- Managing 50+ creators without proper tools/processes
- Not reinvesting in top-performing creators
- Treating influencer marketing as separate from the rest of the marketing mix
