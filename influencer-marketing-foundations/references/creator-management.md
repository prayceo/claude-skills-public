# Creator Relationship Management — Deep Reference
*Source: Sandy Diao, Influencer Marketing Foundations course, 2024*

## Building a Creator Program

### The Creator Relationship Lifecycle

```
Discovery → Outreach → Negotiation → Briefing → Content Creation →
Review → Publishing → Measurement → Relationship Development → Repeat
```

---

## Phase 1: Discovery

### Finding the Right Creators

**Manual discovery methods:**
- Platform search (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube) using relevant hashtags
- Competitor analysis: who are competitors partnering with?
- Community exploration: who do your existing users follow?
- Industry events and podcasts: who speaks to your audience?

**Tool-assisted discovery:**
- Influencer platforms (CreatorIQ, Grin, Aspire, Upfluence)
- Social listening tools (Brandwatch, Sprout Social)
- Affiliate networks with creator programs

**Vetting checklist:**
- [ ] Audience demographics match target user profile
- [ ] Engagement rate is above platform average for their tier
- [ ] Comment quality shows genuine audience interaction
- [ ] Content style and values align with brand
- [ ] No brand safety concerns in recent content history
- [ ] Follower growth pattern is organic (no suspicious spikes)
- [ ] They haven't promoted direct competitors recently

## Phase 2: Outreach

### The Outreach Framework

**Subject:** Short, personalized, non-spammy

**Message structure:**
1. **Personal hook:** Reference specific content they created that you genuinely liked
2. **Brand intro:** One sentence about who you are and what your product does
3. **Why them:** Specific reason they're a good fit (not "we love your content" — be specific)
4. **The ask:** Clear description of what you're proposing
5. **Next step:** Simple call to action (reply, schedule call, etc.)

**Response rate benchmarks:**
- Cold outreach: 5-15% response rate
- Warm intro: 25-40% response rate
- Inbound (creator applies to your program): 60-80% response rate

### Building Inbound Creator Interest
- Create a visible "partner with us" page on your website
- Share creator success stories publicly
- Engage with potential creators' content before reaching out
- Build a reputation as a brand that treats creators well

## Phase 3: Negotiation and Compensation

### Compensation Models

**Flat fee:** Fixed payment per deliverable
- Pros: Simple, predictable cost
- Cons: No performance alignment
- Best for: Awareness campaigns, content licensing

**Performance/affiliate:** Commission per conversion
- Pros: Aligned incentives, scalable
- Cons: Creators prefer guaranteed income; may attract lower-tier creators
- Best for: Acquisition campaigns with clear conversion tracking

**Hybrid:** Base fee + performance bonus
- Pros: Creator gets security + performance incentive
- Cons: More complex to manage
- Best for: Balanced campaigns seeking both reach and conversions

**Product/experience:** Free product, exclusive access, or unique experiences
- Pros: Low cash cost, creates genuine product advocates
- Cons: Limited to creators who genuinely want your product
- Best for: Early-stage companies, building long-term ambassadors

### Rate Negotiation Guidelines
- Always start with your budget range, not their rate card
- Factor in content usage rights (repurposing adds value and should cost more)
- Consider exclusivity carefully (expensive and often unnecessary)
- Package deals (multiple posts over time) typically offer better rates
- Performance data from previous collaborations strengthens negotiation position

## Phase 4: Content Management

### The Brief-to-Publish Process

**1. Brief delivery** (Day 0)
Send detailed but not over-prescribed brief. Include:
- Key talking points and required mentions
- Platform-specific requirements (duration, format, hashtags)
- Tracking link and/or promo code
- Examples of content style you like (from other creators, not competitors)
- What NOT to say (compliance, competitor mentions, etc.)

**2. Content draft review** (Day 3-7)
- Review for accuracy and brand safety only
- Don't over-edit — creator voice is the point
- Maximum 2 rounds of revisions (specify in contract)
- Turn around feedback within 24 hours

**3. Publishing** (Day 7-14)
- Agree on posting time (consider audience time zones)
- Creator posts on their account
- Brand engages (like, comment) within first hour
- Monitor comments for questions or concerns

**4. Content collection** (Day 14-21)
- Collect final content files for repurposing
- Screenshot/record performance metrics
- Request creator's analytics screenshot for verification

## Phase 5: Measurement and Optimization

### The Creator Performance Dashboard

Track per creator:
| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| Reach | How many people saw the content |
| Engagement rate | How compelling was the content |
| Link clicks | How many took action |
| Conversions | How many became users |
| CPA | Cost efficiency |
| Content quality score | Subjective rating (1-5) for repurposing potential |
| Relationship score | How easy to work with (responsiveness, quality, flexibility) |

### Creator Segmentation

After 3+ campaigns, segment creators into:

**A-tier (re-engage immediately):** High performance, easy to work with, great content
- Offer ambassador/long-term deals
- Increase budget allocation
- Explore co-creation opportunities

**B-tier (optimize and retry):** Moderate performance, potential to improve
- Adjust brief, try different content angle
- Test on different platform/format
- One more chance before moving to C

**C-tier (phase out):** Low performance or difficult to manage
- Don't renew
- Redirect budget to A and B tier creators
- Document learnings for future selection

## Building an Ambassador Program

### When to Launch

Launch an ambassador program when:
- You've tested with 10+ individual creators
- You know which creator profile performs best
- You have repeatable processes for management
- You're ready to commit to ongoing relationships

### Ambassador Program Structure

**Typical structure:**
- 10-30 creators in ongoing partnership
- Monthly content requirements (2-4 posts)
- Monthly compensation (fee + performance bonus)
- Exclusive perks (early access, product input, events)
- Quarterly review and renewal

**Program benefits for creators:**
- Guaranteed income stability
- Early access to new features
- Input on product development
- Community with other ambassadors
- Brand association and credibility

**Program benefits for brand:**
- Consistent content calendar
- Deeper product understanding = better content
- Cost predictability
- Stronger creator loyalty
- Content library growth

### your product Ambassador Program Concept

**"your product Brand Voices"** — 15-20 niche creators who integrate your product into their daily personal content.

**Selection criteria:**
- Genuine wellness practice (not performative)
- Active engagement with personal content
- Audience in your product's target demographic
- Consistent content quality and posting schedule

**Monthly deliverables:**
- 2 Instagram/TikTok posts featuring your product in their daily routine
- 1 longer-form piece (YouTube, blog, podcast) about personal habits
- Authentic mention (not scripted — share real experience)

**Compensation:** $500-2,000/month base + $2-5 per attributed install

## The 4-Factor Creator Selection Model

**Factor 1: Audience Alignment (40% weight)**
- Does the creator's audience match your target customer?
- Look at: demographics, interests, geography, psychographics
- Tools: Platform analytics, HypeAuditor, Modash, CreatorIQ
- Red flag: Large following but audience is mostly bots or non-target demographics

**Factor 2: Content Quality and Style (25% weight)**
- Is the creator's content style compatible with your brand?
- Look at: production quality, storytelling ability, editing, tone
- Red flag: Content quality is inconsistent or doesn't match your brand aesthetic

**Factor 3: Engagement and Authenticity (25% weight)**
- Does the audience actively engage (comments, shares, saves, not just likes)?
- Are comments genuine or generic? Does the creator respond to their audience?
- Red flag: High follower count with low engagement (< 1% on Instagram, < 3% on TikTok)

**Factor 4: Brand Safety and Values (10% weight)**
- Any past controversies? Content align with brand values? Recent competitor partnerships?
- Red flag: Controversial content, frequent negative press, competitor partnerships

### Finding Niche Creators

For specialized products (like a consumer app), niche creator discovery is critical:

1. Search relevant hashtags on each platform (relevant community hashtags, relevant engagement hashtags session, #wellnesscreator)
2. Look at who your existing users follow (survey or social media analysis)
3. Check competitor partnerships (who are similar brands working with?)
4. Attend niche events and conferences (religious conferences, consumer media events)
5. Use your own community (existing power users who create content)
