## Gotchas

Common mistakes to avoid when applying these frameworks:

1. **Don't prioritize follower count over audience alignment:** Sandy's 4-Factor Selection Model weights Audience Alignment at 40% -- the highest factor. A 500K-follower lifestyle creator with 2% target audience overlap is far worse than a 30K niche creator whose followers match your product's target exactly.
2. **Don't over-script creator content:** The #1 campaign mistake Sandy identifies is over-controlling creative. Target audiences especially detect inauthenticity. Give creators key messages and let them translate into their own voice -- a stiff, brand-approved script will underperform authentic testimony every time.
3. **Don't measure influencer ROI on direct attribution alone:** Influencer marketing has significant halo effects (branded search lifts, word-of-mouth, social proof) that tracking links miss. Sandy's Full ROI formula includes halo conversions and content production value -- using only last-click attributed installs will systematically undervalue the channel.
4. **Don't skip the content repurposing step:** Every piece of creator content should be tested as paid creative through whitelisting. For your product, consumer UGC testimonials repurposed as whitelisted ads often outperform brand-produced creative -- failing to negotiate usage rights upfront wastes the most valuable asset.

## Example

**User asks:** "We want to launch an influencer program for your product's seasonal campaign campaign. Where do we start?"

**Approach:**
1. Apply Sandy's value chain (Strategy -> Creator Selection -> Outreach -> Briefing -> Execution -> Measurement) and scope a seasonal campaign with 20-30 micro to mid-tier niche creators over the seasonal campaign period
2. Use the 4-Factor Selection Model to build a creator shortlist -- prioritize creators who already post about daily engagements, daily habits, or content learning, and verify audience demographics match your product's core (US, 25-55, active)
3. Design a "40 Days of Engagement" campaign brief with a primary message, 2-3 talking points, and creative freedom for creators to share their authentic seasonal campaign engagement journey using your product
4. Output a launch plan including: creator tiers and compensation structure, outreach timeline (start 8 weeks before seasonal campaign), tracking setup (unique promo codes + UTM links per creator), and repurposing plan for whitelisted ads

## Core Philosophy

> "Influencer marketing isn't about finding famous people to talk about your product. It's about finding authentic voices whose audience trusts them, and creating partnerships where the creator's authentic expression of your product drives genuine action."

Influencer marketing has matured from a brand awareness tactic into a full-funnel acquisition and retention channel. The companies that win at influencer marketing treat it as a systematic, measurable channel — not a one-off PR exercise. The shift from "paying for reach" to "investing in authentic advocacy" is what separates effective influencer programs from wasted spend.

## Module 1: Influencer Marketing Fundamentals

Influencer marketing is increasingly important due to paid social fatigue (CPMs rising 15-30% annually), a trust shift toward creators, algorithm favor for creator content, and the content supply benefit (repurposable UGC). The value chain runs: Strategy → Creator Selection → Outreach → Negotiation → Briefing → Content Creation → Review → Publishing → Amplification → Measurement. Most companies under-invest in strategy and measurement.

**ROI peaks in the micro to mid-tier range** (10K-500K followers) — balancing engagement rate with reach. Five partnership models exist: one-off sponsored posts, ambassador programs, affiliate/performance-based, UGC production (non-posting), and whitelisted/spark ads. Key anti-pattern: treating influencer marketing like traditional advertising rather than authentic creator partnerships.

## Module 2: Creator Selection and Outreach

Creator selection is the highest-leverage activity. Use the **4-Factor Selection Model**: Audience Alignment (40% weight), Content Quality/Style (25%), Engagement/Authenticity (25%), Brand Safety/Values (10%). A great creator match matters more than a large following.

**Outreach process:** Build a 5x prospect pipeline (reach out to 50 for every 10 you want), personalize outreach referencing specific content, qualify via media kit review, and negotiate on total package (fee + usage rights + whitelisting). Usage rights are often the most valuable component.

For niche products like your product, find creators through relevant hashtags (relevant community hashtags, relevant engagement hashtags session), existing user follows, competitor analysis, and industry events.

## Module 3: Campaign Design and Execution

A great **campaign brief** covers: background/objectives, key messages (one primary sentence + 2-3 talking points), creative direction (format, tone, references), logistics (deliverables, timeline, usage rights, payment), and tracking (UTM links, promo codes, disclosure). The #1 mistake is over-controlling creator content — give key messages and let creators translate into their authentic voice.

**Repurpose every piece** 3-5 ways: organic post, whitelisted/spark ad, brand organic reshare, paid creative, and website/app store social proof. Campaign types include launch campaigns (5-15 creators in 1-2 weeks), always-on programs (10-50 creators monthly), seasonal campaigns (your product: seasonal campaign, seasonal event, Advent, Christmas), and challenge/trend campaigns.

*→ Full detail: `references/platform-tactics.md`*

## Module 4: Measurement and Scaling

Measure at three levels: **content metrics** (per post: reach, engagement, CTR), **conversion metrics** (per campaign: attributed installs, CPA by creator, revenue), and **program metrics** (quarterly: total revenue, blended CPA vs. other channels, content production value). Attribution is challenging due to delayed effects and cross-platform viewing — mitigate with unique promo codes, UTM links, branded search monitoring, and self-reported attribution surveys.

**Full ROI** = (Direct conversions + Halo conversions + Content production value) / Total spend. Scale through four stages: Test (5-10 creators, $5-20K/month) → Optimize (double down on winners, $20-50K) → Scale (25-50+ creators, ambassador program, $50-200K) → Mature (core channel at 10-30% of budget, $200K+).

## Decision Tools

### Decision 1: Should We Invest in Influencer Marketing?

```
Are your paid social CPAs rising >15% year over year?
├── Yes → Influencer marketing can provide cheaper creative and new audience access
│   └── Do you have a product that benefits from personal recommendation?
│       ├── Yes → High priority to invest
│       └── No → Consider UGC production only (no organic posting)
└── No → Is your category one where trust/authenticity matters?
    ├── Yes → Influencer marketing builds trust at scale
    │   └── Start with micro-influencer test
    └── No → Lower priority, focus on other channels first
```

### Decision 2: Which Influencer Tier Should We Focus On?

```
What's your primary goal?
├── Direct acquisition (installs, subscriptions)
│   └── Focus on micro to mid-tier (best CPA)
│       └── 10-30 creators with niche audience alignment
├── Brand awareness
│   └── Focus on mid-tier to macro (reach + credibility)
│       └── 3-5 larger creators with broad appeal
├── Content production (for paid ads)
│   └── Focus on nano to micro (cheapest, highest volume)
│       └── 20-50 creators producing UGC-style content
└── Community building
    └── Focus on nano (most engaged, most authentic)
        └── Build ambassador program with loyal users
```

### Decision 3: How Should We Structure Creator Compensation?

```
Is the creator an established professional with >50K followers?
├── Yes → Fixed fee + usage rights + potential performance bonus
│   └── Performance bonus tied to: installs or subscriptions via tracked link
└── No → Is the creator a genuine product user/fan?
    ├── Yes → Product gifting + small fee + affiliate commission
    │   └── Convert to paid partnership if content performs well
    └── No → Fixed fee per piece of content
        └── Start small, increase if ROI is proven
```

## Applied to your product

your product has an exceptional opportunity with influencer marketing given the niche creator ecosystem.

**The Niche Creator Ecosystem:** niche creators are a large, engaged niche on Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. These creators have highly engaged audiences who trust their recommendations, especially for products that align with their values. The authenticity requirement is especially high — a influencer who recommends a mobile app must genuinely use and believe in it.

**Creator Selection Strategy:** Focus on micro to mid-tier niche creators (10K-200K followers) who create content about daily engagement, daily practice, content learning, or mindful living. These creators have the highest engagement rates and most trusted recommendations in this niche. Look for creators who already practice daily engagements or daily habits — they can authentically integrate your product into their existing content about personal growth.

**Campaign Strategy:** Start with an ambassador program of 10-15 niche creators who genuinely use your product. Give them free premium subscriptions + modest compensation for monthly content. The content should show their actual use — morning daily routine, engagement during the day, sharing insights from the daily content. This authenticity is critical for target audiences.

**Seasonal Amplification:** Scale the program around religious calendar peaks. For seasonal campaign (40 days before seasonal event), launch a "40 Days of Engagement" creator campaign with 20-30 creators journaling their seasonal campaign engagement practice using your product. This type of structured, time-bound campaign creates urgency and community.

**Content Repurposing:** Every piece of creator content should be tested as paid creative. Authentic UGC testimonials ("How this app changed my daily practice") are likely to be the highest-performing ad creative for your product's audience. Whitelisted ads through creator accounts will combine creator credibility with paid reach.

**Measurement:** Use unique promo codes per creator and tracking links. Supplement with self-reported attribution during onboarding to capture the halo effect of creator recommendations that don't click through a tracking link.

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Influencer Tier | Classification of creators by follower count (nano, micro, mid, macro, mega) |
| Whitelisted Ads | Paid ads run through a creator's account, combining authenticity with paid reach |
| Spark Ads | TikTok's whitelisted ad format that promotes organic creator content as paid |
| UGC (User-Generated Content) | Content created by real users or creators (vs. brand-produced) |
| Creator Brief | Document outlining campaign objectives, key messages, and creative direction for creators |
| Usage Rights | Legal permission to use creator content in your own marketing (paid ads, website, etc.) |
| Ambassador Program | Ongoing creator partnership with regular content cadence and deeper brand relationship |
| Affiliate Model | Compensation structure where creators earn commission per conversion they drive |
| Engagement Rate | Interactions (likes, comments, shares) as a percentage of reach or followers |
| Content Repurposing | Using influencer content across multiple channels (organic, paid, website, app store) |
| Halo Effect | Indirect conversions driven by influencer exposure but not tracked through attribution |
| Branded Content Ads | Meta's format for running paid ads through a creator's Instagram or Facebook account |
| Media Kit | Creator's document showing audience demographics, engagement stats, and past partnerships |
| FTC Disclosure | Required disclosure (#ad, #sponsored, #partner) when content is paid or gifted |
