As you undertake this task, do it with the understanding that a detailed, comprehensive, and complete analysis is not just preferred, but required, to ensure that the decision-making process is informed by the most accurate and relevant information possible. The following instructions are designed to help you perform a break-even analysis for the new product line, incorporating the supplied cost and revenue information.

1) Begin by determining the fixed costs associated with the new product line. Fixed costs are those costs that do not change with the level of production or sales, such as rent, salaries, and equipment depreciation.

2) Identify the variable costs per unit for the product. Variable costs are those that vary directly with the level of production, such as materials and labor.

3) Calculate the contribution margin per unit, which is the price at which the product will be sold minus the variable cost per unit. The contribution margin reveals the portion of sales revenue that is not consumed by variable costs and contributes to covering the fixed costs.

4) Use the contribution margin to compute the break-even point in units, which is the number of units that must be sold to cover all fixed and variable expenses. Accomplish this by dividing the total fixed costs by the contribution margin per unit.

5) Assess different sales volume scenarios where the units sold exceed the break-even point, to estimate the level of profit for each scenario.

6) Incorporate sensitivity analysis to examine how changes in the key assumptions, such as variable costs, fixed costs, and product price, affect the break-even point. Show how these possible changes can impact profitability.

7) Provide a thorough risk analysis that examines potential external and internal factors that could affect the costs or sales of the new product line.

8) Offer strategic recommendations based on your findings, including whether the break-even point is achievable based on current market conditions and internal capabilities.

9) Present the data in a clear and organized manner, ensuring that all calculations are laid out logically so the outcomes can be easily followed and understood.

10) Summarize the analysis in a final report that communicates the critical findings and strategic implications to decision-makers who may not have a financial background. Utilize visual aids such as charts or graphs to enhance the clarity of the data presented.
