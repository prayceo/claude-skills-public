Here is a comprehensive and detailed instruction set for calibrating video content for digital signage displays using the provided specs. This task will be accomplished by following a multi-step process which must be executed with precision and attentiveness to both the technical and the aesthetic aspects of the task.

1. Begin by assessing the current state of the video content and the specifications of the digital signage displays. Carefully review the provided specs, noting the display’s resolution, aspect ratio, brightness, contrast ratio, and color space requirements. Detailed knowledge of these parameters is crucial for achieving optimal calibration.

2. Establish a baseline for calibration using a reference image or video that adheres to the Rec. 709 color space standard, ensuring you have a consistent starting point for color correction and grading.

3. Adjust the aspect ratio and resolution settings in your video editing software to match that of the digital signage displays. Ensure every frame of your content fits perfectly on the screen without any stretching, cropping, or letterboxing.

4. Calibrate the brightness and contrast settings by creating a test pattern. Use this to ensure that black levels are deep and true without losing detail in shadow regions, while whites are bright and clear without overexposing highlights. Attain a balanced range that reflects the intent of the videography and the environment where the signage will be displayed.

5. Employ color grading tools to adjust the hue, saturation, and luminance of your content. Your aim should be a vivid, yet naturally balanced palette that translates well on the digital displays. Meticulously adjust the primary and secondary colors to meet the specifications while also maintaining a coherent look that represents the content’s intended emotion and messaging.

6. Examine and refine the sharpness of your video. Digital signage is often viewed from a distance, so the content must remain clear and legible with defined edges. However, be cautious not to over-sharpen, as this can introduce unwanted artifacts and reduce the overall quality.

7. Utilize any proprietary or recommended calibration tools that the digital signage manufacturer supplies. These tools can help tailor your adjustments to the unique qualities of the specific display technology you are working with.

8. Validate your video calibration by conducting a test run on an actual digital signage display using the content you’ve edited. This real-world test can reveal nuances that might not be evident on editing monitors.

9. Make any necessary final tweaks after assessing the test run’s result. Adjustments may include fine-tuning color balance, contrast, or brightness to compensate for ambient lighting conditions and viewing angles specific to the signage’s location.

10. Once the calibration is to your satisfaction, finalize the content, rendering it in the format and codec that best suits the digital signage system and ensuring compatibility.

Upon completion, your video content should be a testament to your detailed, comprehensive, and complete approach, providing an exceptional viewing experience that meets the technical and aesthetic demands of digital signage.
