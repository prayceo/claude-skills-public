Your task is to conceptualize and lay out a strategic plan for an e-learning module that addresses the critical area of conflict resolution skills tailored to a remote work setting.

Begin by undertaking a detailed instructional analysis to define the core objectives that this e-learning module must achieve. Ensure that these objectives are SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Consider the characteristics and needs of the remote workforce, such as asynchronous communication challenges, cultural diversity, and the absence of non-verbal cues, along with the usual factors like language proficiency and technology accessibility.

3. An in-depth analysis of common conflict scenarios that occur in remote settings, accompanied by interactive case studies which allow learners to apply conflict resolution theories.

4. A comprehensive discussion of cultural sensitivity and how it plays a crucial role in preventing and resolving conflicts across a globally dispersed team.

5. Integration of video tutorials featuring experts in the field of conflict resolution and remote work dynamics. Here, you can illustrate nuanced strategies through engaging narratives and expert insights.

6. Creation of an assessment plan that includes quizzes, reflective journals, and peer-to-peer evaluations to measure the learners’ understanding and application of the conflict resolution skills.

7. Suggestions for incorporating user-generated content, like discussion forums or group projects, to foster a community learning environment where experiences and strategies can be shared.

8. Recommendations for the use of asynchronous and synchronous tools that facilitate the discussed conflict resolution methods. Describe how these tools can be used in various scenarios.

9. A blueprint for the deployment of this e-learning module on the chosen Learning Management System (LMS), taking into account the technical capabilities and limitations of the platform in delivering the interactive components of the course.

10. Craft draft email communications to relevant stakeholders explaining the module’s objectives, benefits, and requirements for successful implementation.

Conclude the module design process by outlining a detailed evaluation plan that utilizes Kirkpatrick’s Four-Level Training Evaluation Model. The plan should illustrate how to measure reaction, learning, behavior, and results post-module implementation, ensuring alignments with the initial objectives you’ve established.

Throughout the creation of this e-learning module, maintain a consistent, engaging narrative, utilizing various multimedia elements to cater to different learning styles. Also, ensure that all instructional materials are accessible to learners with disabilities, adhering to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG).

Your instructional design plan should be evidence-based and grounded in the latest research on conflict resolution and remote work dynamics. Be explicit about the pedagogical underpinnings for your choices and the expected learning outcomes. Use detailed, comprehensive and complete descriptions to allow for a complete absorption of the material for the learner, enabling them to apply what they’ve learned effectively in their remote work environment.
