Document your findings in terms of the ecological footprint, total greenhouse gas emissions throughout the lifecycle, water usage, and resultant air and soil pollution. Contrast these impacts against the environmental costs of traditional waste disposal methods.

Finally, draft an execution plan for your findings. Outline the recommended actions for piloting the waste-to-energy project, including stages for technological installation, testing, optimization, and scaling. Be thorough in addressing risk management steps, fail-safes, and contingency measures.

Remember, during your analysis and planning, prioritize sustainable practices and waste reduction at the source as the foremost goal, with energy recovery as a beneficial secondary aim.

Maintain clarity and specificity in your recommendations, ensuring that the strategies proposed adhere to scientific principles, sustainability goals, industry best practices, and stringent ethical standards. Be detailed in your projections, and comprehensive in the scope of impacts considered, while adhering closely to the data and context provided.
