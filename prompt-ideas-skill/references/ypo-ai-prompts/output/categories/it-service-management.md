1. **Catalog Current Services:**
   – Compile a comprehensive list of all current IT services, mapping them against business functions they support.
   – Note the technology stack, the service lifecycle stage, and any dependencies associated with each service.

2. **Assess Service Utilization:**
   – Calculate detailed metrics on the utilization of each service. Analyze logs to gauge peak usage times, user demographics, and performance data.
   – Check against Service Level Agreements (SLAs) to ensure they are being met and consider the user satisfaction levels for each service.

3. **Evaluate Business Alignment:**
   – Analyze how each IT service aligns with the broader business goals and priorities.
   – Create a comprehensive evaluation report detailing the relevance of each service from a strategic standpoint.

4. **Identify Redundancies and Gaps:**
   – Look for services with overlapping functionalities and mark them for potential consolidation.
   – Identify gaps where business needs are not being met by existing IT services, suggesting areas for potential service development.

5. **Prioritize Services:**
   – Assign a priority level to each service based on its business importance, usage metrics, and strategic value.
   – Sort services into categories for maintenance, improvement, development, or decommissioning.

6. **Develop a Rationalization Plan:**
   – Propose a detailed, actionable plan for the rationalization of the IT service portfolio based on the prioritized list.
   – Consider the implications of service changes on operational workflows, staffing needs, and budget constraints.

7. **Cost-Benefit Analysis:**
   – Conduct a thorough cost-benefit analysis for the rationalization plan, weighing the financial implications against expected gains in efficiency and productivity.
   – Document a detailed breakdown of the financial impact of each proposed action within the rationalization plan.

8. **Risk Management:**
   – Prepare a comprehensive risk assessment for proposed changes to the service portfolio.
   – Propose risk mitigation strategies for each high-risk element identified in the rationalization plan.

9. **Policy Recommendations:**
   – Suggest policies and procedures to support the ongoing rationalization and maintenance of the service portfolio.
   – Include guidelines for new service introduction, existing service assessment, and the decommissioning process.

10. **Stakeholder Communication:**
    – Draft outlines for communication to stakeholders detailing the rationalization plan, its expected benefits, and the changes they may encounter.
    – Include strategies for managing change within the organization, emphasizing the anticipated improvements and support structures.

11. **Implementation Roadmap:**
    – Provide a detailed timeline for the implementation of the rationalization plan, including critical milestones, responsible parties, and estimated completion dates.
    – Ensure that the implementation phases are logical, feasible, and minimally disruptive to ongoing operations.

12. **Monitoring and Evaluation Framework:**
    – Suggest a detailed framework for the monitoring and evaluation of the rationalization process post-implementation.
    – Define key performance indicators and set benchmarks for success and service quality maintenance.

Please note that throughout this process, the focus remains on a detailed evaluation and strategic planning approach, aimed at maintaining alignment with business objectives and improving operational efficiency. Any proposed changes must be in line with the current technological capabilities and future IT trends while focusing on sustainability and adaptability.
