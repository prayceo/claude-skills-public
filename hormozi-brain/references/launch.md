## The Core Thesis

> **Optimize for ROAS, not CPL.**
> The $5 CPL cohort was worth ~$20 each. The $17 CPL cohort was worth ~$189 each.
> Cheap leads are the trap. Expensive leads are the profit.

Everything in this playbook flows from this principle: **add friction to filter, pay more per lead, get more per customer.**

> "I'm willing to pay more for leads that are worth more." — Hormozi

> "When you add friction to a funnel, all of our costs go up, but our ROAS typically also goes up."

## The Money Model Structure

Every launch should have **three revenue moments** so 30-day cash exceeds acquisition + delivery cost:

```
ATTRACTION OFFER  →  UPSELL/OTO  →  CONTINUITY
(Self-liquidator)    (High-ticket)   (Monthly recurring)
```

> **"Create 100 ways to offer your product, not 100 products."** [$100M Money Models, p.155]

### 1. Attraction Offer (Front-End)
- **Pattern:** "Pay Less Now / Pay More Later" + giveaway
- **Purpose:** Self-liquidating acquisition; raises show rates
- **Price anchor:** $6K front-end (fits within 70% of SMBs' available credit — research-backed)
- **Mechanic:** Bonus stack where each bonus is a standalone reason to buy

### 2. OTO / Upsell (Back-End Margin)
- **Placement:** Thank-you page only. One-click button. No checkout form.
- **Price:** 3× the front-end (e.g., $6K → $18K = $6K now + 3×$1K/mo)
- **Proof:** 11-minute video with case studies and numbers
- **Scarcity:** Limited cohort (mirrors quality concern, not manufactured)
- **Target conversion:** 4–6% of buyers (5% is healthy)
- **Result:** Becomes meaningful margin to buy more reach

### 3. Continuity (LTV Amplifier)
- Recurring subscription layered after OTO
- Example: $299/month ACQAI after $3K one-time

## The Six Launch Components

### 1. OFFER
- "Pay Less Now / Pay More Later" + "Buy X Get Y Free"
- **Each bonus must be a standalone reason to buy.** Litmus: could I sell this bonus on its own at the full price I'm claiming?
- Anchor → stack → snap (drop price, then restack)

### 2. OPT-IN
- **Two-step**: button first, then form pops
- Collect full data (business owner status, annual revenue) — filters tire-kickers
- **Accept 30% opt-in rate** over 50%+ if quality improves
- Counterintuitive truth: higher CPL with higher ROAS beats lower CPL with lower ROAS

### 3. EMAIL
- Curiosity themes, if/then "don't paths," abandoned carts, VIP pushes, terminal countdowns
- **Volume escalation:** Low frequency 45 days out → exponential increase 24 hours out → blitz on launch day
- Hormozi sent **43 million emails in 6 weeks**; deliverability dropped 2 points and recovered
- **Campaign architecture:** Separate tracks for every objective (register / upgrade VIP / become affiliate / attend / buy OTO)
- **Decision tree logic:** "If didn't register → CTA to register. If registered, didn't upgrade → CTA to upgrade. If upgraded → CTA to affiliate."
- [$100M Playbook Fast Cash, p.13, p.17]

### 4. ADS
- **"Content is the targeting."**
- **Bank 100–300 creatives pre-launch**
- Favor net-new looks; heavy carousel + UGC mix (outperforms polished ads)
- Test cold-traffic headlines (winner example: *"This book is so good, it should be illegal"*)
- Test warm-traffic headlines (winner: *"Get more customers, spend more money, less time"*)
- Reuse proven angles across platforms with format adaptation

### 5. AFFILIATES
- **~20% of topline revenue; remove it and total drops ~25%** (compounding lift)
- Two-tier prize structure: everyman rewards + top-performer rewards
- Daily leaderboards (competition drives effort)
- Proven creatives + swipe files provided in "affiliate blackbook"
- Rising-star outreach: personally reach out to last year's top 50–100 by name
- **Incentive ladder** (actual Hormozi structure):
  - **10+ referrals** = Lost Chapters bonus + Affiliate Day access
  - **10+ attendees brought** = VIP tier for free (gets them invested)
  - **Top 10** = in-person breakfast/dinner + personal acknowledgment
- Top affiliates get a **personal phone call from leadership**

### 6. WEBINAR
- **"Reveal price early. Restack. Ask again — without being a dick."** [$100M Money Models, p.89]
- Script every word (Hormozi's was **1,700 slides, word-for-word scripted**, at 185 WPM)
- Structure: free value stack → **pattern interrupt** → donation/paid stack

## The Two-Stage Webinar (Script Engineering)

### Stage 1: Free Value Stack (~35 minutes)
1. **Stakes first (15–20 sec):** "We're breaking two Guinness World Records"
2. **Proof of stakes:** Live judges on stage for verification
3. **Bold promise repeated:** "Get more customers, spend more money, less time" (restate multiple times)
4. **Damaging admission:** *"I have a conflict — this is how I make money"* (admission builds credibility)
5. **Credibility layer:** "Top 15% of 51K public companies by revenue"
6. **Pivot:** Shift focus from presenter to viewer ("enough about me, about you")
7. **Objection preempt:** *"This won't work for me"* → answered with 3–4 angles
8. **Problem stack:** Pain → more pain → more pain, with **emotive specificity** ("restful weekend," "hot showers," "family dinners" — never generic "free time")
9. **Inversion proof:** Show what kills a business (no cash flow) to prove cash flow matters
10. **Yes stack:** Series of increasingly permissive yes questions
11. **Recap slide:** Compress entire argument into 5 statements
12. **Transition to pitch:** "Who wants to master it?"

### Transition: Pattern Interrupt (~6 minutes)
- **Physical reset:** Hormozi took off his hat mid-webinar, read a handwritten letter, then replaced the hat
- Signals emotional reset and reframes audience segment (freebie-seeker → buyer-mindset)
- **Close the loop** on non-buyers before addressing buyers

### Stage 2: Donation / Paid Stack
13. **Bonus stack structure** (per bonus item):
    - What is it
    - Why it matters
    - Why doing it right is important
    - What's inside
    - Imagine having it
    - What it cost *me* to create
    - What *you're* getting
14. **Price reveal:** Anchor high ($100K–$1M) → drop to reasonable → drop further → tease freemium → add bonuses → **restack** all bonuses → drop price again
15. **CTA #1:** "Available now"
16. **CTA #2:** Scarcity (e.g., 25K units for 3M people)
17. **CTA #3:** Show logistics ("go.acq.com")
18. **CTA #4:** Transition to next offer (OTO)
19. **OTO brief:** 11-min video proof → one-click button
20. **FAQ overlay:** Address internationals, payment plans, credential concerns
21. **Objection arc:** *"You're about to hit a roadblock. Most people give up. But not you because…"*
22. **Final proof:** Show the work (e.g., 2 years of consulting notes, 226 businesses tracked)
23. **Close:** Reiterate mission, thank you, repeat key action

### Framing: Mission Over Transaction
> "Help me put 200 books in hands of others."

Donation framing > product framing. Same ask. Different frame. Reduces friction on big ask.

## The Scarcity Stack (Hormozi's "Never Publicly Documented" Frame)

Don't announce scarcity once. **Stack it across multiple frames:**

1. "Lots of people will want this"
2. "It's affordable"
3. "Check out logistics"
4. "Speed matters"
5. "Here's bad news — we only made X units"

Each frame adds cumulative pressure. Single-mention scarcity underperforms by design.

---

## The Restack (Critical — Most People Skip This)

> **"You have to restack every time. You don't want to do it. You feel like it's weird. You got to do it. It does not have the psychological effect unless you recreate the contrast and then rebring it back down."**

**Mechanic:**
1. List every bonus
2. Build deprivation (what life looks like without it)
3. Drop price
4. **Restate every bonus again**
5. Rebuild deprivation
6. Drop price again

Without restacking, the psychological effect of a price drop is **40%+ weaker**. Audience must re-experience the contrast cycle.

> "Motivation is always equal to opposite of deprivation. The pain is the pitch."

## The Meta-Proof Stack

One proof type can be dismissed. Layer them so none survives dismissal:

- **Social proof** (client quotes, case studies)
- **Statistical proof** (70% of SMBs have $10K+ credit)
- **Third-party verification** (Guinness judges live on stage)
- **Direct proof** (your own business numbers)
- **Portfolio proof** (companies you've invested in)
- **Meta-proof** (showing the work — 2 years of consulting, 226 businesses)

## High-Ticket OTO Mechanics

**Placement:** Thank-you page only. Not email. Not popup.
**Entry:** One-click button (checkout already authenticated).
**Proof:** 11-min video with case studies + numbers.
**Price:** 3× front-end (ex: $6K offer → $18K commitment = $6K now + 3× $1K/mo).
**Scarcity:** Limited cohort (genuine quality concern, not fake).
**Target:** 5% of buyers. Only want high-conviction.
**Integration required:** You must have the back-end capacity to deliver — don't offer what you can't run.

## Email Cadence Architecture

```
45 days out  ─── Low frequency, value emails
30 days out  ─── Warm-up, teasers, curiosity opens
14 days out  ─── Announce launch, early-bird/VIP push
 7 days out  ─── Daily cadence, scarcity hints
 3 days out  ─── Multiple sends/day, objection handling
24 hours out ─── Exponential — blitz
Launch day   ─── Hourly pulse, countdown, restacks
Post-launch  ─── 48-hour close window, final ask
```

**Send discipline:** Maintain value cadence (e.g., Hormozi's Mozi Minute 2×/week) *all year* so deliverability reserve exists when you need to burn it.

> **"The reason we have a deliverability score is so that when it comes time to ask, we can ask."**

> "Go big or don't go at all. When you do all the work and set everything up right, you have to be willing to make trades."

## Open-Loop Curiosity Hook

Don't name the bonus up front. Create an open loop that stays unresolved across every email until the pitch moment:

> "Better than an NFT, less than a Bitcoin."

Hormozi used this instead of naming "Lost Chapters" directly. Works across all audience segments because imagination fills the gap.

## Audience Segmentation at the Top

Label audience segments explicitly early so:
- Non-target audience feels addressed ("you're taken care of")
- Target audience feels **called out personally**

Example: *"Y'all are my ballers. These are my two people."*

## The Frequency-Beats-Perfection Rule for Asks

> **"The salesperson who asks someone to buy more times will close more deals. Period."**

Overcomes matter less than ask frequency. **Ask 4 times with decent objection handling > ask 1 time with perfect objection handling.**

> "As long as you believe in it, because you know more about the context than they do, then you should maximize the likelihood that they make a good decision. That's fundamentally persuasion — the difference between that and manipulation is that manipulation hurts them."

## War Stories (Use for Credibility)

- **$5 vs. $17 CPL story** — headline proof of the friction-forward thesis
- **35 internal reps vs. 750 outsourced facilitators** — internal did same revenue at 1/20 the headcount (18× per-person outperformance). "Quality always beats quantity when it comes to talent."
- **YouTube throttle recovery** — $30+ CPL for a week at $100K/day spend; eventually normalized. Don't kill campaigns early; feed the platform more data.
- **TikTok shutdown battle** — couldn't scale 17.8× ROAS channel because platform kept shutting down the account despite "vanilla" creative. Platform risk is real.
- **Deliverability drop and recovery** — sent 43M emails, deliverability dropped 2 points, fully recovered post-launch. Built goodwill all year with Mozi Minute to cash in.
- **Two-year AI training project** — $135K × 226 businesses + 1,000 prior consulting records to train ACQAI. "Unscalable work to build something that scales."
- **The $99 vs. $9 pricing confusion** — said "drove price down from $99 to only $9" on slides. Audience thought they deserved the $99 tier for $9. Better: "For the first time ever, we created a $9 option." Clarity lesson.

## Anti-Patterns (Don't Do This)

1. **Chasing cheap CPL over ROAS.** Inverts the right lens. Pay more per lead, get more per customer.
2. **Too many weak bonuses.** "Better to have fewer better than more weaker. Only one of them will push the consumer over the edge." Litmus: could this bonus sell on its own?
3. **Switching all content to promotional mode.** Keep normal cadence *plus* promotional. More work, but maintains audience trust.
4. **Over-explaining bonuses.** Compress chapters into 2–3 sentence bullets. People don't want why — they want what + why-it-matters.
5. **Single-layer proof.** Stack proof types; don't rely on one.
6. **Skipping the objection arc.** Address anticipated friction mid-pitch. Missing this loses 10–20% of audience.
7. **Dropping price once (no restack).** 40%+ weaker effect. Always restack.
8. **Assuming people know you exist.** "No one knows you exist. No one cares." The first person hearing you for the first time is the 2,000th time you've said it.
9. **Deliverability anxiety.** "The guys who have built brands are usually the ones who are more scared." You built the reserve — use it.
10. **Skipping the pattern interrupt between free and paid.** 6 minutes of transition closes the loop on non-buyers so buyers can hear the pitch.
11. **Underestimating time investment.** "Even with six weeks, it was a multi-year project that culminated." This isn't a 60-hour launch. It's 2 years of prep + 60 hours.
12. **Testing novelty over mastering basics.** "What makes you advanced isn't that you're advanced. It's doing all the basics that you already know every time, all the time at scale."

## Psychology & Persuasion Mechanics (Why It Works)

| Mechanic | Principle |
|---|---|
| **Contrast & deprivation** | Motivation = opposite of deprivation. Show the gap with emotive specificity. |
| **Repetition baseline** | First-time listener hears what you've said 2,000 times. Diminishing returns are still returns. |
| **Restack cycle** | Bonus stack without restack = punishing but reinforcing. Restack = tension release. |
| **Damaging admission** | Admit your conflict/bias. Makes everything else more credible. |
| **Proof stacking** | Single proof = dismissable. Layered proof = undeniable. |
| **Inversion** | Show what kills the business (no cash flow = death). Creates urgency. |
| **Yes stack** | Incremental yes-answers prime neural compliance for the final ask. |
| **Future pacing** | Imagination activation beats feature lists. |
| **Price anchoring** | Absurdly high anchor makes reasonable price feel like a win. |
| **Latency kills** | Speed crushes. Tiny-now > big-later almost always. |
| **Ask frequency > objection perfection** | More asks > better asks. |
| **Language segmentation** | Explicit labels make both in-group and out-group feel addressed. |
| **Mission framing** | "Help me put books in hands" > "Buy my book." Same ask, different frame. |
| **Willingness-to-pay research** | 70% of SMBs have $10K+ credit. Price accordingly. Not guessed. Researched. |

## Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Deliverability dip during blasts | Maintain year-round value cadence; list hygiene; warmed sends. [$100M Playbook Fast Cash, p.17] |
| Ad platform throttles | Rep escalation; channel backups; blended CAC approach |
| Attribution gaps | Blended ROAS dashboard; channel lift triangulation |
| Compliance issues | "State the facts. Tell the truth." Real proof. Real claims. |
| Ops/support spikes | Staff contractors (Hormozi hired 90 chat reps + 750 facilitators) |
| Finance cash-in timing | Plan for large inflow + refund window |
| Legal | Update TOS, guarantee language, prize rules before launch |

### Alternatives Rejected
- **Organic-only:** Too slow. Algorithm-gated.
- **Single-channel:** Fragile. Platform risk.
- **Low-friction CPL chase:** Hurts ROAS, intent, show-up. "Optimize for returns, not cheap leads." [$100M Money Models, p.159]

## When to Apply This Playbook

**Strong fit:**
- Book, course, cohort, or high-ticket product launch
- You have an audience (email list, followers, affiliate network) — this is a multiplier, not a zero-to-one strategy
- You can afford $25K+ ad budget and 8-week timeline
- You have a back-end (OTO, continuity) to capture margin

**Weak fit:**
- First-ever product with no audience (build audience first)
- Low-ticket single-SKU commodity (continuity-only model fits better)
- Service business with fulfillment bottlenecks (scale ops first)
- Timeline pressure under 8 weeks (compress at your own risk — "you won't")
