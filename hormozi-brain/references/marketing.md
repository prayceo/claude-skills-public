# Alex Hormozi Marketing & Lead Generation Mental Models

## SIX WAYS TO GET CUSTOMERS

Every business has exactly six ways to acquire customers. Most businesses only use one or two. The goal is to eventually use all six.

1. **Paid Ads** - Buying attention (Facebook, Google, YouTube, TikTok ads). Fastest to turn on, but costs money upfront.
2. **Earned Media (Organic Content)** - Creating content that platforms distribute for free. Slower to build, but compounds over time.
3. **Owned Media (Email / CRM / Phone)** - Communicating with people you already have contact info for. Highest ROI channel because you already "own" the audience.
4. **Manual Outbound** - Cold calls, cold emails, cold DMs. Cheapest to start, requires time not money. "Warm outreach first, then cold."
5. **Affiliates / Referral Partners** - Other businesses or individuals who send you customers in exchange for a commission or reciprocal benefit.
6. **Word of Mouth / Referrals** - Existing customers telling others. The byproduct of delivering exceptional value. Cannot be "hacked" — only earned.

**Key insight:** Channels 1-4 are things YOU do. Channels 5-6 are things OTHER PEOPLE do for you. The most scalable businesses eventually get most of their customers from channels 5 and 6.

## CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITION (CFA)

The single most powerful growth engine. Instead of funding growth from savings or investors, you fund it from your customers' first payment.

### Core Equation
```
30-Day Cash Collected > 2x (Customer Acquisition Cost + Cost of Fulfillment)
```

> "This is what allowed us to go from zero to $2.4 million a month in 18 months running 74% net margins."

### The CFA Cycle (Example)
1. **Borrow $100** in ad spend
2. **Acquire 2 customers** at $50 CAC each
3. **Each customer spends $300** in the first 30 days
4. **Fulfill at $50 per customer** ($100 total)
5. **Result:** $600 revenue - $100 ad spend - $100 fulfillment = **$400 cash in hand + 2 ongoing customers**
6. **Reinvest** the $400 into acquiring more customers
7. **Repeat** — growth accelerates every 30-day cycle

### Why CFA Matters
- You never need outside capital to grow
- Growth is limited only by how fast you can reinvest
- The business funds its own scaling
- You maintain 100% ownership
- Cash flow positive from day one of scaling

### How to Achieve CFA
- **Increase front-end pricing** (higher initial payment)
- **Decrease CAC** (better ads, better offers, better conversion)
- **Decrease fulfillment cost** (systems, leverage, group delivery)
- **Speed up collection** (charge upfront, not monthly)

## THREE NUMBERS YOU MUST KNOW

If you don't know these three numbers, you're flying blind.

### 1. CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) — All-In
Not just ad spend. EVERYTHING it costs to acquire a customer:
- Ad spend
- Sales team compensation
- Marketing tools/software
- Content creation costs
- Commission on referrals
- Divide total by number of new customers acquired

### 2. LTV (Lifetime Value) — Gross Profit, NOT Revenue
```
LTV = Gross Profit per Month / Monthly Churn Rate
```
Or equivalently:
```
LTV = (Price - Cost of Fulfillment) / Monthly Churn Rate
```
**Critical distinction:** LTV is calculated on GROSS PROFIT, not top-line revenue. A customer paying $500/month with $400 in fulfillment costs has an LTV based on $100/month, not $500.

### 3. 30-Day Cash
How much cash a customer puts in your pocket in the first 30 days. This is what funds your growth engine (CFA).

### Key Ratios
| Ratio | Target | What It Means |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| LTV : CAC | > 3:1 | For every $1 you spend acquiring a customer, you get $3+ back over their lifetime. Below 3:1 means you can't afford to scale. |
| 30-Day Cash : CAC | > 1:1 | You recoup your acquisition cost within 30 days. This enables CFA. Below 1:1 means you're funding growth from reserves. |

### The Hierarchy
- **LTV:CAC > 3:1** = Viable business
- **30-Day Cash:CAC > 1:1** = Self-funding growth
- **Both** = Unlimited scalable growth without outside capital

## THE DATA-DRIVEN MARKETING FRAMEWORK (Three Wheels)

The same data can be framed in radically different ways by spinning three variables:

1. **% of clients** — What portion of customers achieve the result
2. **The outcome** — What specific result they achieve
3. **Time duration** — Over what time period

### Example
Same underlying data, different framing:
- "50 seven-figure gyms this year" (okay)
- **"Every 7.7 days we create another seven-figure gym"** (far more compelling)

Both statements are identical mathematically. The second is dramatically more powerful because it implies speed and inevitability.

### How to Apply
Take your results and spin the three wheels to find the most compelling true statement:
- Change the denominator (per day vs. per year)
- Change the percentage (top 10% of clients vs. average)
- Change the outcome metric (revenue vs. profit vs. customers vs. units)
- Change the time window (weekly vs. monthly vs. annually)

**Rule:** Every version must be 100% true. You're not fabricating — you're selecting the most compelling frame for real data.

## CONTENT MARKETING (Five Phases)

Content is a long game. Most people quit at Phase 1. The real payoff starts at Phase 3.

### Phase 1: Post Something
- Just start. Quality doesn't matter yet.
- Get over the fear of putting yourself out there.
- Learn what creating content feels like.

### Phase 2: Post Consistently
- Pick a cadence and stick to it.
- Build the habit and the muscle.
- Start learning what resonates.
- **Phases 1-2 = Lead NURTURE** (warms up people who already know you)

### Phase 3: Post on ALL Platforms
- Take the same content and distribute everywhere.
- YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, podcast.
- Repurpose — don't recreate from scratch.
- **Phase 3+ = Lead GENERATION** (reaches new people who don't know you yet)

### Phase 4: Max Out All Platforms
- **5-10 short-form pieces per day** across platforms
- **1-2 long-form feed posts per day**
- Volume is the unlock. Most people dramatically under-post.
- Algorithm rewards volume + consistency.

### Phase 5: Create AND Capture
- Content now generates measurable leads and revenue.
- Build systems to capture attention into your owned media (email, phone, CRM).
- Content becomes a true acquisition channel, not just brand building.

### Key Content Principles
> "No such thing as too long, only too boring."

- Length doesn't kill engagement — boredom does.
- A 60-minute video that's riveting outperforms a 30-second video that's dull.
- Their content operation: **~$70K/month investment** generates **~$2M/month equivalent** in what it would cost to buy the same reach through paid ads.
- That's roughly a **28:1 return** on content investment.

## FOUR WAYS USERS BRING TRAFFIC

The most scalable products have users who bring in OTHER users. Four mechanisms:

1. **Built-in Viral Sharing** — The product naturally encourages sharing (e.g., "Share your results," referral bonuses, social features). Using the product = telling others about it.

2. **Users Embed Product on Their Sites** — Users put your product/widget/badge on their own websites, creating backlinks and visibility (e.g., "Powered by [Your Brand]" badges, embeddable tools).

3. **Users Create SEO Content** — Users generate content that ranks in search engines (e.g., reviews, forum posts, user-generated pages, templates others search for).

4. **Users Pay (Enabling Traffic Buying)** — Revenue from users funds paid acquisition, creating a virtuous cycle. More users = more revenue = more ad spend = more users.

**Best case:** Your product has all four. Each one multiplies the others.

## LEAD NURTURE (Four Ways)

Getting a lead is not enough. Most leads are not ready to buy immediately. Nurture keeps you top-of-mind until they are.

1. **Email Sequences** — Automated value-delivery emails. Not pitches — genuine education and proof. Drip over weeks/months.

2. **Retargeting Ads** — Show ads to people who already visited your site or engaged with content. Cheapest form of paid advertising because the audience is warm.

3. **Content** — Regular content on social media that leads see in their feeds. Constant, passive reminders that you exist and deliver value.

4. **Community** — Facebook groups, Slack channels, forums, events. Creates belonging and peer pressure. People in communities buy at higher rates and stay longer.

**The principle:** Nurture is not selling. Nurture is depositing value so that when you do make an offer, the "trust bank account" is full.

## AD HEADLINES FRAMEWORK

Every headline must pass three tests before running:

### Test 1: "Would I Read This?"
- Put yourself in the prospect's shoes.
- If YOU wouldn't stop scrolling for this headline, neither will they.
- Be ruthlessly honest.

### Test 2: "Is It Specific?"
- Vague = ignored. Specific = compelling.
- Bad: "Grow your business fast"
- Good: "Add $47K in monthly revenue in 90 days without hiring"
- Numbers, timeframes, and concrete outcomes create specificity.

### Test 3: "Does It Create Curiosity or Promise Value?"
- **Curiosity:** Makes them NEED to know more (open loops, unexpected claims, pattern interrupts)
- **Value:** Promises a clear, desirable outcome they want
- Best headlines do BOTH — promise value while creating curiosity about HOW.

### Application
Run every headline, subject line, hook, and thumbnail through all three tests. If it fails any one test, rewrite it. This simple filter eliminates 90% of weak marketing before it ever runs.

## THE PROSPERITY VS. PRESERVATION DOCTRINE

Marketing messaging must match the economic mood. What works in good times fails in bad times, and vice versa.

### Normal Economy (Growth/Prosperity)
- **"Gain" messaging outperforms "savings" messaging 2:1**
- People are optimistic, aspirational, growth-minded
- Lead with: opportunity, upside, growth, expansion, ambition
- Example: "Scale to 7 figures" / "Double your revenue" / "Unlock your next level"

### Recession Economy (Fear/Preservation)
- **"Savings/preservation" messaging outperforms "gain" messaging 4:1**
- People are fearful, protective, survival-minded
- Lead with: protection, savings, security, efficiency, keeping what you have
- Example: "Protect your margins" / "Cut costs without cutting quality" / "Recession-proof your business"

### Recession-Specific Strategy
> "Sell to the masses, not the classes."

During downturns:
- Lower price points win (more buyers, less risk per purchase)
- Volume beats premium
- "Save money" beats "make money"
- Essential beats aspirational
- The wealthy still buy, but the mass market shifts dramatically

**Tactical implication:** Be ready to pivot messaging (not necessarily product) when economic conditions shift. Same offer, different frame.
