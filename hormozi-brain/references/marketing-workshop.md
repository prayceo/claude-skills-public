## The Core Thesis

> **"No one knows you exist. No one knows your business exists. No one knows what you're up to. Everyone's obsessed with their own lives."**

This is the frame. Most businesses' #1 problem isn't market saturation — it's that the market doesn't know they exist yet.

**The Jennifer Lawrence math:**
- $50M ad spend = ~5B impressions = "famous"
- Cut that down to your local/national pond, but the math scales
- "You can either grind or pay, but you get there either way"

**The leak test (proof of invisibility):**
Hormozi's $100M Leads book accidentally went live on Amazon for 24 hours a month before launch. Only 72 people bought. His takeaway:

> "It would take someone to actually find the book AND start advertising it. I was shouting from the rooftops and 19 out of 20 followers didn't know I had a book."

If you think you've "saturated" your market — you haven't.

## Funnel Math Reality — Multiple Paths Up

Before pouring effort into one lever, understand the math. A simple funnel:

```
$1 CPC  →  10% opt-in  →  10% schedule  →  10% buy
```

- **Improving ANY single step by 50% = 50% more revenue**
- **Improving TWO steps (e.g. opt-in 10% → 20% AND schedule-to-buy 10% → 50%) = 10× business**

**Implication:** There are many equally effective paths up the mountain. Better ads OR better conversion OR better offers all multiply the same bottom line. Pick the path with the **highest risk-adjusted return based on your existing skills and resources.**

## The 3 Marketing Pillars

> Every successful marketing campaign rests on three things. If any one is missing, the others can't save you.

1. **VOLUME** — massive, unrelenting quantity
2. **CLARITY** — specific, third-grade messaging in the customer's language
3. **PROOF** — compelling, stacked evidence that the thing works

**The diagnostic:** If your marketing isn't working, one of these three is the leak. Don't bolt on new tactics. Fix which of the three is broken.

> "If the facts aren't compelling, you don't warp the facts — you warp reality by generating more proof."

### The Midwit Meme (Clarity Principle)

- **Beginner:** "Just tell people what you have clearly"
- **Midwit:** "How many seconds should my ad be? What color should the button be?"
- **Expert:** "Just tell people what you have clearly"

**Be specific over general. Clear over clever. Deletion often beats explanation.**

### The Proof Hierarchy

1. **Show what only you can show**
2. **Say what only you can say**
3. **Build proof *before* you market** (every business Hormozi has started began with proof collection)
4. If you can stand next to 20,000 5-star reviews, you don't need to say much

> **"Proof is still underrated. You need to show more, document more, and show more than you currently are."**

**Action item:** Go get proof first. Then marketing becomes 10× easier.

## More Better New — The Volume Framework

### The Rule
**99% of the time, the answer is MORE or BETTER. Rarely NEW.**

- **More** = increase volume of what already works
- **Better** = leverage — take existing output further
- **New** = only when market is genuinely maxed, platform is shrinking, or you have no existing traction

### The Accordion Process
Volume → insights → better → more volume at higher quality → more insights → …

Start with **more**. It's the risk-adjusted best move, and it naturally reveals the paths to *better*.

### When to do NEW
- Market genuinely maxed out (more common locally than nationally)
- Platform shrinking (newspaper ads, dying channels)
- You have zero existing traction to amplify

### The <$1M Rule
> "For businesses earning less than a million dollars annually, the answer is almost always MORE."

Volatility in revenue = insufficient daily activity. Raise the minimum standard.

## The 6 Improvement Buckets (Lead Generation)

### Bucket 1: Better Offer / Lead Magnet

Three differentiation vectors — apply any combination:
- **Faster** — "What outcome is possible in 1/3 the time?"
- **Easier** — "What can we pre-do for them?" (turnkey onboarding, pre-built systems)
- **Less Risky** — warranties, guarantees, insurance policies covering biggest fears

**Attraction offer wrappers** (from Money Models):
- Giveaways (call runner-ups with a "second place prize")
- Win your money back (service businesses)
- Decoy offers (transactional businesses)
- Pay less now, pay more later
- Buy free — give away more value than they pay for, adjust pricing accordingly *(check legal)*

### Bucket 2: More Ads

Specific targets:
- **National businesses:** 1–5 ads per $1,000 monthly spend
- **Local businesses:** 5–10 ads per $1,000 (higher fatigue)
- Example: $10K/mo local = **50–100 ads/month = ~25/week**

### Bucket 3: Better Ads (Hooks & Creative)

**Hooks are 80% of the battle.** Good hook lasts years in a new market.

### Bucket 4: Better Targeting

Progression ladder (each level more accurate):
1. Interest/demographics
2. 1% lookalike from opt-ins (needs ~1,000 data points)
3. Lookalike from calls
4. Lookalike from sales
5. Lookalike from high-value customers (needs ~1,000 upsell records)

#### The Vista PE 4-Step Customer-Profiling Process

Vista Private Equity ($300B fund) uses this to triple company value in 3 years. **80/20 logic applied recursively** — know customers better than they know themselves, cut streams bringing low-value customers, 5× investment in streams bringing the top 20%.

**Step 1 — Demographics (Who are they?)**
Age, location, company size, role. Example: Gym Launch found *"1 employee + signed lease + 30+ members = 5× more likely to succeed."* Identify the hard filters.

**Step 2 — Quantifiable Requirements (What are they doing?)**
Measurable behaviors/characteristics. Make these **requirements** (not preferences) upfront. Screen for them in intake.

**Step 3 — Activation Behaviors — Pre-Purchase (What did they do before buying?)**
What did your best customers *consume* before they bought?
- Hormozi's data: **78% of customers consumed 2+ long-form pieces** before buying
- Application: **Lead magnet + webinar/VSL = ~2–3 hours of consumption** → can sell expensive things to this engaged cohort
- **Strategy:** *force* cold leads through the same consumption pattern

**Step 4 — Activation Behaviors — Post-Purchase (What predicts success?)**
Example: *First high-ticket upsell within 7 days = success predictor.* Build this trigger into onboarding.

**Step 5 (bonus) — Channel Analysis (Which sources bring best customers?)**
Some channels/lead magnets attract higher-LTV customers. Hormozi example: scaling roadmap lead magnet vs direct workshop offer. **Double down on highest-LTV sources.**

### Bucket 5: Platform Expansion
- **B2C:** Meta, YouTube, TikTok
- **B2B:** Meta, YouTube, LinkedIn, X
- Add channels only after existing channel is well-optimized. Most people are on most platforms.

### Bucket 6: Brand as Multiplier
Brand isn't a separate bucket — it amplifies all 5 above. Same CPL, same conversion rate, much higher LTV and referral rate.

## Lead Magnet Templates (3 Types)

### Type 1: Problem Revelation
*"Here's a problem you didn't know you had."*
- Example: "7 mistakes homeowners make when fixing their HVAC"
- Best for: cold/unaware audiences

### Type 2: Trial of Solution
*"Try a piece of what you'd buy, for free."*
- Example: Free trial of local map ranking service
- Best for: solution-aware audiences who need to validate

### Type 3: Splinter Offer (One Step of Multi-Step)
*"The first 1/4 of what the full offer provides."*
- Solve 1/4, not 90%. Leave room for the paid offer.
- Example: Scaling roadmap → paid scaling workshop

### Anti-Pattern
Lead magnets that solve too much. Scaling roadmap example: direct-to-workshop ads had higher upfront ROAS, but the lead magnet had **higher long-term ROAS and much better scale** — because it reached solution-aware, not just most-aware, audiences.

**Insane discount play:** 80–90% off on a small, low-cost component of the offer. The discount attracts; the upsell process is where margin is made.

## Ad Creation Playbook (7 Sources)

### 1. Lifecycle Ads
Document customer journey: sales call → onboarding → check-in → result. Record, montage, publish.

### 2. In-Person Events
Client appreciation events engineered for testimonial capture. Frame events as ad-collection moments, not sales moments.

### 3. Community / Review Site Scrapes
Screenshot reviews from Google, Yelp, Trustpilot, Facebook → each review = one ad. Counter-play: Liquid Death ran 1-star reviews as ads ("we're not for everybody").

### 4. Testimonial Competitions
- Customers submit testimonial videos
- Grand prize for best testimonial
- **Winner is the one that actually converts as an ad** (let the algorithm decide)
- Provide script template + example of what "great" looks like
- Legal: disclose incentivized content

### 5. Delivery / Outcome Moments
Unboxings, physical product delivery, milestone achievements, before/afters. Require a short testimonial in exchange for bonuses/recognition.

### 6. Organic + CTA
Take best-performing organic content, add CTA overlay, run as paid. Engagement already validated by algorithm = cheaper delivery.

### 7. UGC (User-Generated Content)
- Engage "zealots" (highly satisfied customers) to create testimonials
- Quid pro quo arrangements
- B2C example: TikTok Shop affiliate model
- **Influencer whitelisting:** run ads from influencer accounts to your offers

## Hooks & The Kaleidoscope Process

### The Kaleidoscope
Find a winning ad. Make 100+ variations by changing one or two variables per version:
- Actor
- Background
- Speed (1.2× / 0.9×)
- Font
- Colors
- Clothing
- Timing / pacing
- Music

**Example:** Single gym ad (Kale & Maggie) → 100 variations → **$10M in revenue**

### Hormozi's Hook Creation Process (Weekly)
1. **Rewatch top-performing ads** (your own winners)
2. **Review swipe file** — new inspirations you've saved
3. **Write 50+ hooks**
4. **Create 3–5 value pieces**
5. **Test combinations** (pair hooks × value pieces × CTAs)

### Hook Research Tools
- **1of10.com** — packaging & hooks
- **Facebook Ads Library** — free
- **TikTok Creative Center** — free
- SimilarWeb, SEMrush, APS — paid

## Schwartz's 5 Stages of Awareness — Hook Patterns

| Stage | Audience State | Hook Type | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Most Aware** | Knows your product, needs a deal | Offer-driven | "50% off our best-selling product this week" |
| **Product Aware** | Knows you sell it, unsure if right for them | Proof-driven | "Why 10,000 business owners chose us over [competitor]" |
| **Solution Aware** | Wants outcome, doesn't know your product solves it | Promise-driven | "The fastest way to hit 100 leads/day" |
| **Problem Aware** | Senses the problem, doesn't know solution exists | Pain-driven | "Tired of crash diets that never stick?" |
| **Unaware** | Doesn't even know they have the problem | Curiosity-driven | "The hidden way your business is losing $100K every month" |

**The stalled-ad diagnosis:** When ad spend saturates, it's rarely the market. It's usually that you only have Most-Aware and Product-Aware creative, missing the other 3 stages.

## Platform CTA Mechanics

### YouTube — 5 CTA Spots
1. **Bio link**
2. **Description (above the fold)** — bulk change across all videos
3. **1/3 mark in the video** — Hormozi's clockwork CTA placement (captures larger % of viewers than end-of-video)
4. **Product buttons** — direct Shopify integration
5. **Community Posts** — wildly underused; highly effective for engagement + lead gen

### Instagram
- **Single clear CTA in bio**
- **ManyChat integration** on Reels
  - End Reel with comment prompt ("comment BOOK for the free guide")
  - ManyChat auto-DMs the lead magnet
  - **Hormozi example: one viral post = 20,000 leads**

### TikTok
- **Don't use Linktree** — pick ONE primary promotion
- **B2B high-ticket hack:** direct TikTok DMs to Instagram (IG is the better conversion platform)

### LinkedIn
- **Comments drive algo visibility** — a valuable comment on a relevant post exposes your content to the entire commenter's network
- Example: comment on a 2-like post → becomes a 1,200-like post due to commenter's audience
- **Pin a CTA as the first comment** on high-performing posts (avoids external-link penalty, gets the double-exposure of top slot + likes)

### X / Twitter
1. **Shopify banner integration** for lead magnets (~100 leads/day)
2. **Thread strategy:** 10-point threads with embedded CTAs at points 3–4 and final point (two CTAs, not one)
3. **Comment CTAs** on single posts periodically — don't overuse

## Email — The Highest-Return Platform

> "Email is the single highest-return platform that exists. I should have started sooner."

### The Revenue Math
- **Acquisition.com: 25% of total revenue** from email
- **25–50% of revenue is achievable** for most businesses taking email seriously
- Adding email can ~double total revenue for most businesses
- Volume: Acquisition.com sends **36M+ emails/month**

### The 5-Part Email Structure

1. **Subject Line — Build Brand Recognition**
   - Hormozi's brand: "Mozi Minute"
   - Brand > subject-line hacks. Opens come from brand strength, not clickbait.

2. **Hook — Reward the Open Immediately**
   - Use high-performing tweets as openers
   - Pick "dope as shit" over strictly topical
   - Deliver value before asking for anything

3. **Value — One Specific Actionable Thing**
   - Format: *Here's what they're missing → Here's how it works → Here's an example → Here's the summary*
   - One thing. One specific way. One specific person.
   - Don't cram 3 ideas into 1 email — send 3 emails.

4. **CTA — Clear and Obvious**
   - "Want more of this? Go here."
   - No mystery. No hedging.

5. **PS — Reward Cycle**
   - Meme (the "funnies" of a newspaper)
   - Or an additional CTA
   - Keeps people reading to the end; trains them to return

### Frequency
- **Minimum:** weekly for existing customers
- Hormozi writes 3 emails/week (~2 hours every 6 weeks = ~2 hours/month)
- **Never miss a week** — maintain delivery reserve for when it matters

## The 4 Elements of Influential Content

Every piece of content should advance at least one of these:

### 1. Status — Control of Scarce Resources
- Display visible markers of what your audience wants
- Examples: exits, offices, team size, revenue, accolades
- Hormozi: "$46M exit, $100M launch"

### 2. Power — Say-Do Correspondence
- You give instructions → audience follows → good results happen → more compliance next time
- Example: Huberman's hiccup cure (worked once = increased future instruction compliance)
- Parents have power because thousands of childhood instruction cycles resolved correctly

### 3. Credibility — Third-Party Proof
- Awards, metrics, outside verification, media mentions
- Quantifiable > claimed

### 4. Likeness — Visual / Values Similarity to Avatar
- Hormozi: flannel + beard = "hardcore lumberjack" following
- Be yourself → attract people like you
- **Diversity strategy:** include different people in content to attract broader avatars

## The 6-Step Brand Building Process

### Step 1: Determine the Perfect Avatar
Not your *current* avatar. Look across all customers, find the ideal one, name them, profile them completely. Reverse-engineer.

### Step 2: Figure Out What They Want / Suffer From
Not "make money" or "lose weight" — chunk down to **100+ micro-problems and micro-desires** that lead to the main outcome.
- Content goldmine: morning routines, specific foods, first workout without soreness
- Go granular

### Step 3: Make Content That Helps Them Get It
- Give instructions (builds power through say-do correspondence)
- When stuck: think smaller, zoom in, focus on margins
- Deliver implementable tactics

### Step 4: Show How You Have What They Want
- **Status** — display scarce resources
- **Credibility** — third-party proof
- **Likeness** — visual/values similarity
- All three working together

### Step 5: Ask If They Want Your Help (CTAs)
CTAs across all content. Must actually ask for the business.

### Step 6: Do This as Much as You Can
- Without getting bored
- Without letting negative comments stop you
- "Motivation comes and goes quickly. Capture moments of high motivation."

### Minimum Viable Brand
**1–3 posts/week** across your main channels. Shows you're alive and legitimate.

### Hormozi's Actual Content Calendar
- YouTube: 3 shorts/day + 1–2 longs/week
- Instagram: 3 posts/day
- TikTok: 4 posts/day
- LinkedIn: 1 post/day
- Facebook: 3 posts/day
- Threads: 2 posts/day
- YouTube Community: 1 post/day
- X: Variable
- **35,000 pieces/year** (Hormozi cited this during the book launch)

### Native Format Rule
Start in the format that's easiest for YOU to produce:
- Writer → X first
- Talker → podcast first
- Video person → video first
- **Lowest friction = highest consistency**

## BANT Qualification (Detailed)

### The 4 Questions
- **Budget** — Can they afford it? ("Our ticket is $5,000, you okay with that?")
- **Authority** — Can they make the decision? (Target owners/CEOs, not assistants)
- **Need** — Do they actually have the problem? (Pain points in their content)
- **Timing** — Is now the right time?

### High-Converting Timing Qualifier
> *"Would you be willing to start as soon as possible, vs next week?"*

Hormozi's client: **95% of sales came from "as soon as possible" answers**. He immediately disqualified everyone who said "next week."

### The Friction Principle
Adding qualifying friction on intake forms **rarely drops volume, always increases quality**. Hormozi: strategic friction increased workshop net revenue significantly.

## AI-Powered Outreach (5-Step Assembly Line)

### Three Applications of Outreach (Ranked by ROI)

Most founders only use outreach for #1. The higher-ROI plays are often ignored.

1. **Direct customer acquisition** — most common, lowest ROI per touch
2. **Affiliate / partner recruitment** — higher leverage (reach people who already have your customers)
3. **Talent acquisition** — **highest ROI** (employees often more valuable than individual customers)

Pick the application first. The mechanics below work for all three, but the target list and messaging differ.

### Step 1: Lead Generation
- **B2B:** LinkedIn (Sales Navigator)
- **Local:** Google Maps scraping
- Methods: buy, scrape, or manually hunt

### Step 2: Personalization (AI + Data)
Formula: **specific observation + relevant compliment + transition to business**

Data sources:
- Recent LinkedIn posts
- Company news/funding/hiring
- Social media activity
- Website changes
- Door-to-door observations (nice lawn, red door)
- AI summarization scales this

**Goal:** Don't look automated while being automated.

### Step 3: Qualification (BANT on Intake)
Pre-qualify upfront. Predictive models get better with more data fields.

### Step 4: Contact at Scale
- **Power dialers** — max 2–3 lines (Hormozi uses 2-line)
  - Reason: don't waste good leads on simultaneous calls
- **Email automation** — mass personalized emails at scale
  - Example: #1 launch affiliate sent 1M+ personalized AI emails
- **Social DM automation** — ManyChat for IG/Meta

### Step 5: Sales Rep Prep (Highest Leverage)
Brief every rep before a call with:
- Recent company news/changes
- Pain points identified in pre-work
- Previous touches/interactions

**Impact:** Makes rep sound informed vs. "another cold caller" → converts stranger into "someone they know" → polite tone activates.

### AI-Core vs AI-Enabled
- **AI-enabled** = using off-the-shelf tools
- **AI-core** = unique internal data driving core operations
- "You cannot have AI without data." Build the data infrastructure first.

## Conference & Event Tactics

### The Speaking Fee Trade (3 Levels)

**Level 1 — Best Outcome (Attendee List):**
- Establish your fee (e.g., $25K speaking fee)
- Waive fee in exchange for the full attendee list
- Equivalent to a ~$250K advertising gift
- Minimal incremental cost to the organizer

**Level 2 — Good Outcome (Email to List):**
- Waive fee to send one post-event email to their list
- They approve email content first
- Send AFTER your talk (after you've been edified)

**Level 3 — Minimum (Survey Close):**
- End the talk with: *"Were these slides helpful?"* → most say yes
- *"Fill out this link to get the slides + free resources"*
- Survey inside asks: *"Just want slides OR want to learn more?"*
- Routes interest into webinar → call → sale

### Booth Strategy (If You Pay)
- **Location:** buy booth on the **RIGHT side of the entrance** (70% of traffic turns right)
- **Mini-podcast setup:** invite other speakers for on-site podcast recordings
  - Draws crowds, elevates your status by association, generates content
- **Giveaway:** "key to the vault" — basket of keys, one works, put the correct key in on the **last day** of multi-day events

### Event Volume Strategy
- "I get ~1/3 of my leads from events. I only do 2 events per year. Doing 1/week = 25× that opportunity."
- More events → better at events → more invitations → compounding

## VIP Checkbox & Pre-Framing

### The Experiment
Added an **optional "VIP ticket" checkbox** to a registration form. The checkbox did nothing functionally.

### Results
- **80–85%** checked the meaningless box
- **6% fewer overall leads** (slight friction)
- **20%+ higher upsell take rate** on subsequent offer
- **Net: +16% earnings per view**
- Leads who checked box: **10–11× more valuable as customers**

### Why It Worked
**Psychological pre-framing** + **commitment & consistency principle**. The checkbox primed a buying identity. That identity persisted into the purchase decision.

### Application
Look for low-friction pre-framing opportunities everywhere — optional checkboxes, micro-commitments, identity-tagging questions.

## Split Testing — Orange / Apple / Banana

### Step 1: Three Wildly Different Concepts
Orange vs Apple vs Banana. Different hooks, angles, value propositions. Pick the winning category.

### Step 2: Three Variations of the Winner
Green Apple vs Red Apple vs Yellow Apple. Permutations of what won, not new concepts.

**Result: 3 × 3 = 9 systematic variations tested**

### Testing Process
1. Start with **percentage split** (not full 50/50) to find directional winner
2. If winner emerges, run **true 50/50 test**
3. If it holds, it becomes the new control
4. Cost = traffic cost + opportunity cost during testing

### Weekly Cadence
- **1–2 tests per week**
- Always **second agenda item** in marketing meetings (after main KPIs)
- **Priority:** lowest-performing step relative to benchmark (highest leverage point)

### IG Reels Rule of Thumb
5 permutations per day limit on a single core content piece (one video × 5 different hooks).

## Elon's Algorithm Applied to Marketing

> Question → Delete → Optimize → Accelerate → Automate

**In order.** Automating a bad process just makes the bad process run faster.

### Media Team Example
- **Problem:** 8-hour upload from "fancy cameras"; 2–3 week YouTube lead times
- **Question:** why fancy cameras if they slow us down?
- **Delete:** killed 80% of post-production bottleneck
- **Optimize:** 60 minutes prep → 18 hours of post-production savings
- **Result:** lead time dropped to **2 days** (from 2–3 weeks)

### Home Flipping Example (Portfolio Company)
- $16–17M/year, cash-flow constrained
- **90-day flip cycle** → asked "why not 30?"
- Initial reaction: "no way"
- After questioning: "we could do 20"
- **Revenue 3×, cash flow problem solved**

### Speed as the Greatest Strategic Advantage
- **Extreme 1:** do nothing, infinite slowness
- **Extreme 2:** move so fast no one has an innovator's advantage (copy anything instantly)
- Strategy enables speed by allocating all resources to the greatest leverage point

## Anti-Patterns (What Hormozi Sees "All the Time")

1. **Targeting only the 3% (most-aware + product-aware)** — missing 97% of the market through absent Unaware / Problem-Aware / Solution-Aware creative.
2. **"We've saturated our market"** — almost always wrong; actually under-targeting awareness stages.
3. **Obsessing over CPL instead of ROAS** — cheap leads are the trap.
4. **Not collecting BANT on intake** — leaving qualification signal on the floor.
5. **Random friction** — strategic friction increases revenue; random friction kills it.
6. **Generic broad-appeal content** — attracts wrong leads. Be specific to avatar.
7. **Not asking for the business** — having CTAs on some posts, not all.
8. **Overestimating platform limits vs. underestimating production capacity** — the limit is you, not the algorithm.
9. **Spending on "fancy" tools without questioning necessity** — Elon's algorithm would delete.
10. **"I'm not a tech person"** — marketing is technical now. Refusing to learn AI tools = handicap.
11. **Lead magnets that solve 90%** — should solve 25% and leave room for the paid offer.
12. **Switching all content to promotional during launches** — keep normal cadence + promotional on top.
13. **Sending to infrequent emails or not emailing at all** — leaving 25–50% of revenue on the table.

## The Restaurant Reality — What Marketing Can't Fix

> **"The restaurant will not succeed if the food sucks."**

No amount of marketing fixes a fundamentally broken product or service.

- **Negative word-of-mouth** travels faster and broader than positive
- **Reputation building** takes consistent execution over time
- **Reputation destruction** can happen very quickly

**If marketing is working and retention isn't** → the problem isn't marketing. It's the product.

## Marketing's Core Purpose — The Supply-Demand Shift

> **Marketing's purpose:** *artificially shift the demand curve in your favor.*

- More people buy at higher prices
- Superior supply-demand economics (fewer competitors on willingness-to-pay dimension)
- Result: **get more for what you put in** — that's the leverage

Everything else is tactics.

## Immediate Action Items Summary (Post-Workshop Checklist)

### For Paid Ads
1. **Audit your offer** — add speed, risk reduction, OR ease as primary differentiator
2. **Install ad collection systems** — lifecycle tracking, testimonial competitions, review scraping
3. **Write 50+ hooks** — target all 5 awareness levels; create 3–5 value pieces
4. **Optimize for ROAS, not CPL** — focus on LTV / customer, not cost / lead

### For Organic Content
1. **10× your content volume** — find your lowest-friction creation method
2. **Fix all CTAs** — YouTube (5 spots), Instagram (ManyChat), platform-specific
3. **Start email program** — weekly value emails, 5-part structure
4. **Content targeting audit** — does it attract your ideal customer?

### For Outreach
1. **Implement BANT** on every intake form — Budget, Authority, Need, Timing
2. **AI personalization** — specific observation + compliment + business transition
3. **Conference strategy** — speaking fee trade for attendee lists or email sends
4. **Weekly split test** — Orange/Apple/Banana methodology

### Universal Actions
1. **Pick your mountain** — Ads, Content, OR Outreach based on existing strengths
2. **10× your definition of "enough"** — whatever you think is a lot, multiply by 10–100×
3. **Build proof first** — document everything, get testimonials at every touchpoint
4. **Start brand building** — minimum 1–3 posts/week showing you're alive and legitimate

### The Meta-Question
> **"Are you trying, or are you moving mountains?"**

- If your family member's life depended on it, what would you actually do?
- Why aren't you doing that level of effort to save your own business?
- Pick one strategy, fully commit, give it appropriate effort to bear fruit
- **Remember: you're going to be right either way** — about whether it works or doesn't.
