# The People Workshop

**Source:** Leila Hormozi's ACQSA People Virtual Workshop (Nov 7, 2025). The authoritative deep-dive on building people systems — culture, founder capacity, talent funnel, management operating system, leadership operating system.

**Relationship to `leila.md`:** `leila.md` covers Leila's operational leadership frameworks (MITs, Monday Hour One, makers vs managers, 4 types of capacity, delegation scripts). This file covers the **people-systems side** — how to build, scale, and operate a team. Load both for complete leadership work.

**When to load this:** Building/scaling a team, diagnosing culture problems, firing/hiring decisions, designing the talent funnel, 30-60-90 onboarding, management cadence, executive compensation (MBOs), Accountability Dial, self-assessing where you are the ceiling.

## The Core Thesis

> **"What people often perceive as a 'people problem' is actually a system, leadership, or talent problem disguised as one."** — Leila

> **"The business didn't need better people. It needed better systems for people."**

Most plateaus are diagnosable. If you're stuck at $X, your operating system is built for $X. Working harder won't take you to 10× — a new operating system will.

---

## The 5 Scaling Frameworks

Leila's five systems for scaling people. Identify which one is the **priority** for your business right now.

1. **Culture as a System** — enforcement, recognition, consequences
2. **The Founder as the Capacity Ceiling** — your company caps at your personal OS
3. **The People Flywheel** — structure enables multiplication, not dependency
4. **The Talent Engine** — treat talent like customer acquisition (funnel, brand, onboarding)
5. **Systems to Scale** — Management OS + Leadership OS

## The $50M / $8M-OS Collapse Story (Why Systems Matter)

The same week Leila hit $50M in revenue (2017/2018):
- Fired/lost the COO
- 3 top performers put in notice **simultaneously**
- Lost 2 major clients who then started **hate Facebook groups** about her
- 30% month-over-month growth while the team was imploding

> **"I had built a business that was at $50 million in revenue, but I had like an $8 million operating system."**

> **"Dude, we have so much debt. Like we have debt in the business. Systems debt, people debt, sales debt, IT debt, finance debt. I can't build stuff fast enough."**

The lesson she repeats constantly:

> **"Growth doesn't fix a broken system. It amplifies them. It multiplies them."**

## Culture as an Enforced System

> **"Culture is what you reward, you recognize, and you tolerate. Everything else is marketing."**

> **"Culture is caught, not taught. People don't care what you say — they care what you do."**

> **"Culture is what you tolerate. The worst behavior you accept becomes the standard for your whole company."**

### The Culture Stack (Built Bottom-Up)

```
        OBSERVABLE BEHAVIORS        ← what people actually do
         RITUALS & SYSTEMS          ← recognition, rhythms, consequences
          DECLARED VALUES           ← the "how"
        PURPOSE & MISSION           ← the "why"
```

**Don't skip layers.** Values posted on a wall with no enforcement = decoration.

### Finding Your Values (Inverse Method)
> "Think about what you detest. Your values are the opposite."

Then build rituals and systems (recognition programs, feedback loops, reinforcement mechanics) to make those values observable.

### Luke Cleaning Up — A Culture Example
A team event, someone tosses a donut, sprinkles go everywhere. Leila walks over and picks them up. The next day Luke (a newer team member) tells her: *"It's cool that you actually walked the talk. I saw you doing that when nobody was watching."*

A month later: the team finishes eating in a conference room, leaves food around. Leila walks in — **Luke is cleaning it up.**

> **"Caught, not taught."**

## The Enforcement Matrix (Performance × Values)

| | **Low Values** | **High Values** |
|---|---|---|
| **High Performance** | **TOXIC TALENT → remove immediately, regardless of impact** | **STARS → promote** |
| **Low Performance** | **EXIT** | **DEVELOP** (people who train 2× longer stay 5–6× longer) |

### The Toxic Salesman Story (Exact Cost)
- Top salesman hitting **150% of quota every quarter**
- Treated sales ops team and everyone else "like garbage"
- Suppressed others to stand out
- Leila postponed firing him to "get through Q4" (big annual bonuses)

**The cost of tolerance:**
- Lost 2 sales ops managers
- Lost 1 head of onboarding
- Lost 2 other sales guys

> **"The million-dollar quarterly quota I didn't want to lose actually cost me $1.2 million in turnover — plus the lost sales from those two sales guys."**

> **"While I was postponing that, what did I teach my team? That performance beats principles."**

### The 3-Person Firing
In December, Leila identified 3 people who "just don't fit." Pulled the leadership team together and said: *"These three have to go. They're not culture fits. I can't sleep at night knowing they're here."*

Leadership pushed back. She did it anyway. **"Everything got better."**

### Non-Negotiable Values Test
Look at your **last 3–5 terminations**. The patterns tell you what you *actually* enforce — regardless of what's on your wall.

## The Recognition Flywheel (The 20%)

Hiring/firing/promoting on values is 80% of culture. The 20% is the recognition loop.

> **"Successful behaviors precede success. Reward the behaviors, not just the outcomes."**

### Recognition Mechanisms
- **Daily:** Slack shoutouts for value-driven behaviors
- **Weekly:** Team celebrations tied to values
- **Monthly / Quarterly:** MVP awards — but tied to **values demonstrated**, not revenue closed

**The trap:** Celebrating only revenue teaches the team you only value revenue. If you want culture, celebrate culture behaviors — visibly, repeatedly.

## Vision as a Retention Tool

> **"A-players don't come and they don't stay for money. They stay for meaning."**

> **"Nobody gives a f*** about your EBITDA."**

A big vision is a **talent magnet** and a crucial sales pitch. Your vision must be **large enough to encompass the personal visions of your team**.

### The Gym Launch Reframe
- **Before:** "Help gym owners grow revenue" (transactional)
- **After:** **"Bring the gym industry from its knees to its feet"** (movement)

Jennifer, one of their operators, had a 3-hour save call with a failing gym owner. Why? Because the vision had framed the mission as *"that person is on their knees — we have to bring them to their feet."*

## The Founder as the Capacity Ceiling

> **"Your company will never outgrow your personal operating system. Bar none."**

A perfect culture + broken founder = capped at $10M. The founder's **emotional capacity, decision-making, and self-awareness** become the growth ceiling.

### The $10M-Stuck CEO Story
Portfolio CEO stuck at $10M for 2 years, asking: *"What should I change? The team? The strategy? The structure?"*

Leila's breakthrough moment:
> **"No. It's you. You're the reason you're not at $100 million yet. It's just you."**

Her diagnosis:
> *"You're still operating this business like you're a $5M founder. You're in every decision. You're the smartest person in every room because you won't hire people smarter than you. You say you want to scale, but you will not let go of control. You're the f***ing bottleneck."*

His breakthrough question:
> **"Who do I need to become?"**

He spent 6 months rebuilding himself. Hired a COO smarter than him. Stopped attending every meeting. Started thinking in years instead of quarters. **24 months later the company was at $100M.**

> **"The skills that got you to where you are are not the skills that you need now."**

> **"Speed without structure is complete chaos."**

### Operator → Architect Transition
The early-stage skills (speed, gut calls, doing all the work, solving every problem) become the trap at later stages. The shift: **stop being the business operator → start being the architect.**

## The Pyramid of Success (John Wooden's Framework)

Rate yourself 1–5 on each trait. Diagnose your lowest foundation before trying to work on the top.

```
                POISE · CONFIDENCE · COMPETITIVE GREATNESS     ← earned, not given
              CONDITION · SKILL · TEAM SPIRIT                  ← "business athlete"
          SELF-CONTROL · ALERTNESS · INITIATIVE · INTENTNESS   ← composure under pressure
           FRIENDSHIP · LOYALTY · COOPERATION                  ← trust before performance
             INDUSTRIOUSNESS · ENTHUSIASM                      ← foundation
```

### Base Layer: Industriousness & Enthusiasm
- **Industriousness:** *full mental engagement toward progress* — not busyness, not long hours. 12 hours of mastering something important > 80 hours of task execution
- **Enthusiasm:** *genuine love for the mission* — not fake positivity. The team's belief is downstream of yours

### Layer 2: Trust (Friendship, Loyalty, Cooperation)
- Friendship: see people as humans outside work
- Loyalty: invest in people when they struggle (if aligned with values and invest in themselves)
- Cooperation: work **with** the team, ask for input, don't treat people as cogs

### Layer 3: Composure (Self-Control, Alertness, Initiative, Intentness)
- Self-control: respond, don't react. The leader must be steady
- Alertness: pattern recognition. Great founders **foresee** problems; reactive founders firefight
- Initiative: teach the team to act like owners. Leaders train people how to interact with them
- Intentness: relentless focus. **Say no to good opportunities if they're not the right ones**

### Layer 4: Business Athlete (Condition, Skill, Team Spirit)
- **Condition:** mental stamina + **strategic rest**. Judgment > output
- **Skill:** constant learning. Avoid stagnation after success
- **Team Spirit = Ego Death:** *"Can you celebrate when someone on your team gets credit for your idea?"* If you need to be the hero, you cap the company at your performance

> Leila's shift: "I remember when I stopped doing sales and missed the high. Then I was like, 'I'm going to get it through them.' Now celebrating their wins is **the best feeling in the world.**"

### Top: Poise, Confidence, Competitive Greatness
- **Poise:** *peace in knowing who you are and how you lead* — not fake high energy
- **Confidence:** derived from **full preparation** — knowing you've put in the work
- **Competitive Greatness:** wanting the hard thing **because** it's hard. Leaning into crises rather than shrinking. This distinguishes good from legendary.

> **"Leadership is not about having all the answers. It's about staying calm and focused and confident when you don't have them."**

## Operationalizing Values in Hiring

Don't ask retrospective interview questions. **Test values live on the call.**

### Competitive Greatness → The Variable Pay Test
When negotiating comp:
> **"Would you like more variable pay, or more base pay?"**

- **Competitively great:** always wants variable pay. *"I want to show you I can win. I'll take the bet on myself."*
- **Not competitively great:** argues for more base, claims to want to "win" but really wants more guaranteed money

> **"Don't tell me. Show me."**

### Sincere Candor → The Red-Flag Feedback Test
Give them hard feedback mid-interview:
> **"This is the one thing my team said is a red flag for you. Tell me why you think they'd say that."**

Watch how they respond:
- Tell the truth?
- Get defensive?
- Own it?

### Unimpeachable Character → The Self-Deprecation Trap
Create psychological safety by throwing yourself under the bus ("nobody's perfect, everyone's got vices…"), then ask about hobbies and weekends.

> *"Oh, now that you said it's okay — let me tell you about snorting coke off a hooker's ass last weekend. Okay, you're not hired."*

**The principle:** It's not what they say. It's how they reveal themselves in the conversation.

### Entry-Level as Clean Slate
> **"How hard is it to take a zero to a 10 versus a negative five to a 10? A lot easier."**

Entry-level people arrive at zero (no bad habits from other orgs). Experienced hires often arrive at negative-five — you first have to unlearn their prior culture.

## The Triangle of Scale

Every organizational problem maps to one of these three corners. Most founders obsess over "people" and ignore the other two.

```
        FUNCTIONS
         (what work needs to happen)
        /              \
   PEOPLE    ━━━━    OPERATIONS
   (right roles)      (how work gets done)
```

### Growth-Stage Problem Map

| Team Size | Typical Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **0–10** | Role clarity | **Job scorecards** (4Rs: Role, Responsibility, Result, Requirement) |
| **10–50** | Information doesn't flow | **Structured cadence** — meetings, comms channels, rhythms |
| **50–100** | Accountability breaks down | **KPIs, OKRs, RACI matrix** — one owner per outcome |
| **100+** | Leadership capacity | **Build missing layers** — executive / middle management |

> Most founders think "I have a people problem" — and what they actually have is an absence of structure.

---

## The Accountability Dial (5 Levels)

Define the required level for each role. Train people to move up. Hold them accountable to the level.

| Level | Behavior | What They Do |
|---|---|---|
| **L1** | Wait to be told | Pure task execution — "tell me what to do" |
| **L2** | Ask what to do | Bring problems, wait for answer |
| **L3** | Recommend then act | Bring solutions, get thumbs up/down |
| **L4** | Act with small decisions | Own a few things, report after |
| **L5** | Full ownership | Inform only if relevant |

**The common trap:** Hiring L2 people for L5 roles — then blaming them for not acting like owners.

### The Question-Answer Framework (Promoting L2 → L3)

Leila's exact script:
> *"Before you come to me, write down three options **and your recommendation**. You tell me the question and the answer you have. I'll give you a thumbs up or thumbs down."*

Stop allowing problems without solutions. Build the decision-making muscle.

### L3 → L4
> *"I want you to own these three things and tell me what you did later."*

### L4 → L5
> *"I trust you. You own this function. Don't need you to report anything."*

## The Talent Funnel (vs. Customer Funnel)

Most companies have world-class customer funnels and broken talent funnels. **They're the same structure.**

```
Applicant Generation      ← job post = sales page; employer brand = marketing
Candidate Experience      ← lead nurture; 24-hr response rule
Interview Process         ← qualification (5 steps)
Closing                   ← sell the mission, sell yourself
Onboarding                ← the "proof" stage
Development               ← retention, growth path
Retention                 ← compound
```

### Stage 1 — Applicant Generation
Job post = sales page. Employer brand = marketing.

> "Speak to A-players by conveying the role's **importance** — not transactional tasks."

### Stage 2 — Candidate Experience
Top candidates leave the market in **8 days**. Average hiring process is **23 days**. **60% lose interest** after 48 hours of silence.

> **"Speed signals excellence. Slowness signals dysfunction."**

**24-hour rule:** every candidate gets a response within 24 hours, even rejections.

### Stage 3 — The 5-Step Interview Process
1. **Screen** — phone / video basic fit
2. **Culture Assessment** — non-negotiables (test live)
3. **Skill Evaluation** — can they actually do the job
4. **Alignment on Offer** — comp, expectations, role clarity
5. **Final Close** — the sell

> "30% of the interview should be them asking you questions. Set expectations. Tell them about the company, role, future."

### Stage 4 — Onboarding (The Proof Stage)

> **"Onboarding is not orientation. It's proof. It's proof you're who you say you are."**

- **91% of employees consider leaving in the first month** when expectations don't match reality
- Most employees aren't at full productivity until **month 6**

**The 30-60-90 Plan** — "promise, proof, promise, proof" repeating:
- Promise what they'll achieve in the next 30 days
- Prove it through deliverables and milestones
- Promise the next 30
- Prove it again

> *"If you're losing people during onboarding, it's probably not because they can't do the job. It's because you didn't set them up to win."*

### Stage 5 — Retention
Retention is cheaper than recruiting. People leave because of:
- Lack of **growth**
- Lack of **pay increase**
- Lack of **recognition**

**Three essentials:**
1. **Clear growth path** — skills needed to reach the next role (not complex titles)
2. **Rhythm of feedback** — weekly 1-on-1s, quarterly reviews
3. **Recognition system** — visible, consistent, values-tied

> **"People don't leave companies. They leave lack of progress."**

## The Management Operating System (EACCR)

Rate yourself 1–10 on each. Total below 30 = "semi-broken" system.

| Practice | Question |
|---|---|
| **E**xpectations | Does everyone know what success looks like in their role? |
| **A**ccountability | Does each outcome have exactly one owner? |
| **C**ommunication | Does information flow up, down, and across without gatekeepers? |
| **C**adence | Are your rhythms (1-on-1s, reviews, syncs) consistent and predictable? |
| **R**ecognition | Are you rewarding the right behaviors, visibly and repeatedly? |

> **"The team mirrors you. If you lack cadence, the company lacks momentum."**

Prioritize your **lowest-scoring two** practices for improvement.

---

## The Leadership Operating System

Rate yourself 1–10. Below 30 = managing, not leading. 30–40 = decent but inconsistent. 40+ = strong foundation.

| Practice | Question |
|---|---|
| **Clear Vision** | Can your team articulate the 5-year picture without you? |
| **Resources** | Are you a resource-gatherer for your team, or a blocker? |
| **Autonomy** | Do people make decisions without asking you? |
| **Long-term Thinking** | How far out do you think? (CEO maturity = 5–15 year horizons) |
| **Rest / Development** | Are you building yourself for the next level? |

> **"The maturity of a founder or CEO can be determined by the time length they speak in."**

## Incentivizing Executives — The MBO Plan

Leila's recommended compensation for C-suite in rapidly scaling businesses:

- **Up to 50% of salary** earned via weighted MBOs (Management by Objectives)
- Weighted mix of:
  - **Personal goals** (skill growth, leadership development)
  - **Revenue targets**
  - **Profitability targets**

**Why MBOs over profit sharing or equity:**
- Profit sharing is volatile in early growth
- Equity is permanent; MBOs can flex with company stage
- MBOs align individual development with business outcomes
- Lets you adjust weights as strategic priorities shift

## Rest as Diagnostic

Leila's first full week off in 6 years, in Newport Beach:
- A key person quit
- The entire ad account got shut off
- **All of it got solved without her**
- Nobody 911'd her

> **"Rest is diagnostic. If you take a break, you can see where systems are weak."**

> **"If you build a business that cannot run without you, you don't build a business. You build dependency."**

**The thought-loop during rest:** your brain expects predictability (work), so when you first rest, intrusive work-thoughts amplify. Tolerate them. Tolerance increases. Eventually they disappear.

> *"Now it doesn't happen at all. If I take a day off, I can just be present. There's no threat to not working."*

## Anti-Patterns (What Leila Sees All The Time)

1. **Hiring for role, not culture first** — Posting on Indeed, hoping good people apply
2. **Slow candidate response** — 60% lose interest at 48-hour silence
3. **One-interview hiring** — single touchpoint = false positives
4. **Not selling in interviews** — A-players have options; if you don't sell, you lose them
5. **Overselling in interviews** — causes the 91% first-month considering-leaving problem
6. **No 30-60-90 plan** — assuming new hires "figure it out"
7. **Focusing only on hiring, not retention** — retention is cheaper
8. **Confusing busyness with industriousness** — 80 hours on tasks ≠ 12 hours on mastery
9. **Hiring L2 for L5 roles, never training up** — creates bottleneck at the founder
10. **Canceling 1-on-1s / inconsistent cadence** — team mirrors you; no cadence = no momentum
11. **Blaming "no talent available"** — A+ talent is everywhere, being recruited by better companies
12. **Hiring execs who do "more of the same"** — you need a strategic thought partner, not another doer
13. **Keeping toxic high performers** — taught the toxic salesman cost Leila $1.2M in turnover
14. **Postponing culture fires** — tolerance redefines your culture to whatever the worst person tolerates
15. **Founder as ceiling** — unwilling to hire people smarter than you, unwilling to let go of control
16. **Operator → still operating at next stage** — speed + gut calls become chaos without structure
17. **Values on a wall without enforcement** — decoration, not culture
18. **Rewarding outcomes only, not behaviors** — gets you outcome-chasing, not values-led teams

## How to Use This Reference

When diagnosing a people/team problem:

1. **Check the frame first** — is this *actually* a people problem, or a system/leadership/talent problem?
2. **Run the 5-framework triage** — which of the 5 scaling systems is the constraint? (Culture / Founder Ceiling / People Flywheel / Talent Engine / Systems to Scale)
3. **Check the Triangle of Scale** — are functions clear, people right-sized, operations working?
4. **Map to growth-stage** — are they solving the right problem for their team size?
5. **Apply the Enforcement Matrix** — who's Star / Develop / Toxic / Exit?
6. **Run the Mirror Exercise** — what behavior frustrates them that they're modeling?
7. **Score Management OS + Leadership OS** — find the lowest two practices; fix those
8. **Test rest** — can the business run without them for a week? If not, dependency, not business.
