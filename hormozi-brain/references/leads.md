# Alex Hormozi: $100M Leads — Lead Generation Operating System

Complete reference of every framework, principle, and tactic from $100M Leads (2022). This is the definitive playbook for getting strangers to want to buy your stuff.

---

## THE CORE PREMISE

Every business problem is a lead problem. If you have enough leads, you can survive bad offers, bad sales, bad fulfillment — long enough to fix them. Without leads, nothing else matters.

The formula: **Offer >> Leads >> Sales**. You need a compelling offer first ($100M Offers), then you need to get it in front of people ($100M Leads), then you need to monetize them ($100M Money Models).

> "You're not getting as many leads as you want because you're not advertising enough."

---

## WHAT IS A LEAD?

A lead is a person you can contact. That's it. Your phone contacts are leads. Your email list is leads. Your social media followers are leads. An **engaged lead** is someone who has shown interest in what you sell — they are far more valuable.

**Lead Magnet** = A free or low-cost offer designed to turn a stranger into an engaged lead. The lead magnet solves one specific, complete problem for free. It should be so valuable that people feel obligated to pay you.

### Lead Magnet Design Rules
1. **Solve one complete problem** — not a teaser, not a taste. Solve it fully.
2. **Make it easy to consume** — the faster they consume it, the faster they trust you.
3. **Give it a compelling name** — a named thing is worth more than a generic thing.
4. **The value should exceed your core offer's price** — if your course costs $500, the free lead magnet should feel like $500+ of value on its own.
5. **Give away the secrets, sell the implementation** — your best ideas should be in the lead magnet. The 99.999% who never buy still spread the word.

## THE CORE FOUR (Advertising Methods)

There are exactly four ways to advertise your business. Every marketing activity falls into one of these four categories. The framework is built on two axes:

**Audience type:** Warm (knows you) vs. Cold (stranger)
**Communication mode:** One-to-One (private) vs. One-to-Many (public)

| | Warm Audience | Cold Audience |
|---|---|---|
| **One-to-One (Private)** | Warm Outreach | Cold Outreach |
| **One-to-Many (Public)** | Post Free Content | Run Paid Ads |

### Sequencing the Core Four
- **If you have more time than money** → Start with Warm Outreach, then Post Free Content
- **If you have more money than time** → Start with Cold Outreach or Paid Ads
- **You only need ONE to get started** — master one before adding the next
- **Max out one channel before diversifying** — most people spread too thin

## CORE FOUR #1: WARM OUTREACH

One-to-one communication with people who already know you. Cheapest, easiest, fastest way to get your first leads.

### Your Warm List
Pull up every contact you have: phone contacts, email accounts, social media connections (Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter). Most people have 500-2,000+ contacts they're not leveraging.

### The Warm Outreach Script Framework
1. **Start with a personal connection** — reference something real about them
2. **Don't pitch** — instead, ask who they know: "Hey [Name], I just started helping [type of person] with [result]. Do you know anyone who might be interested?"
3. **If they say themselves** → offer the lead magnet or set up a conversation
4. **If they refer someone** → warm introduction + repeat

### The ACA Framework (Conversation Pattern)
- **A**cknowledge — Acknowledge what they said
- **C**ompliment — Offer a genuine compliment
- **A**sk — Ask another question to keep the conversation going

This keeps conversations flowing naturally without feeling salesy.

### Key Warm Outreach Rules
- Contact people on the platform where you have the most connections
- Never lead with a pitch — lead with a question or offer of value
- Follow up 2-3 times (most people simply forget, they're not rejecting you)
- **Make the success activities the conditions of your guarantee**: As you get results, note what winning customers did. Guarantee those results on condition new customers do the same things.

## CORE FOUR #2: POST FREE CONTENT

One-to-many communication with your warm audience (and eventually cold audiences via platform distribution).

### Content Value Formula
**Value per second** — every second of content should deliver value. The goal is maximizing how much value someone gets relative to the time they invest consuming it.

### Content Structure: Hook → Retain → Reward
1. **Hook** — Capture attention in the first 1-3 seconds. Pattern interrupt. Bold claim. Specific number. Surprising statement.
2. **Retain** — Keep them watching/reading. Open loops. Stories. Tension. "But here's where it gets interesting..."
3. **Reward** — Deliver on the promise of the hook. Fulfill the curiosity gap. Give them something actionable.

### Content Rules
- **Give in public, ask in private** — your public content is pure value with no ask. Your private messages (DMs, emails) are where you make offers.
- **No such thing as too long, only too boring** — a 60-minute riveting video beats a 30-second dull one.
- **Post consistently before worrying about quality** — volume > perfection in the early phases.
- **Repurpose, don't recreate** — one long-form piece becomes multiple short-form pieces across platforms.

### Content Progression (Maps to Five Phases in marketing.md)
- **Phase 1-2:** Content nurtures your EXISTING audience (warm leads)
- **Phase 3+:** Content generates NEW leads (cold audiences find you via platform algorithms)

## CORE FOUR #3: COLD OUTREACH

One-to-one communication with strangers. A numbers game. The more people you reach out to, the more engaged leads you get.

### Building Cold Lists
Three methods to get targeted lead lists:
1. **Software scraping** — Use outbound lead scraping tools to build lists from LinkedIn, directories, databases
2. **List brokers** — Pay brokers to assemble targeted lists based on your ideal customer profile
3. **Manual scraping** — Manually compile names from industry directories, social media, forums, events

### Cold Outreach Message Framework
1. **Personalized opener** — reference something specific about them (not generic)
2. **Offer something free they'd normally pay for** — lead with value, not a pitch
3. **Keep it short** — under 3rd grade reading level gets 50% more responses
4. **Clear call to action** — one specific thing you want them to do

### Cold Outreach Volume
- **Expect 1-5% of cold contacts to become engaged leads**
- **Contact minimum 100 people per day for 100 days** (The Rule of 100 — see below)
- Most people dramatically underestimate the volume required
- Automate with software where possible, but personalize the first line

## CORE FOUR #4: RUN PAID ADS

One-to-many communication with cold audiences. Reach is guaranteed — but getting your money back isn't. It's a game of efficiency.

### Paid Ads Framework
1. **Start with the entire world as your audience** (haystack)
2. **Narrow down to find your ideal buyers** (needles)
3. **Test creative ruthlessly** — the ad creative matters more than the targeting
4. **The 70/20/10 Rule**: 70% of budget on proven winners, 20% on variations of winners, 10% on wild new tests

### Ad Creation Formula
Every ad needs four elements:
1. **Hook** — stops the scroll (bold claim, specific number, pattern interrupt)
2. **Problem** — agitates the pain they're experiencing
3. **Proof** — shows that you or others have solved this problem
4. **Call to Action** — tells them exactly what to do next

### Paid Ads Rules
- The top 0.1% of advertisers test dramatically more ads than average
- **Never judge an ad by clicks** — judge by cost per engaged lead and cost per customer
- Start with one platform, master it, then expand
- **Retargeting is the cheapest form of paid advertising** — show ads to people who already engaged with your content

## THE RULE OF 100

The foundational volume commitment that makes everything else work.

> Every day, do ONE of these:
> - Reach out to 100 people (warm or cold outreach)
> - Post 100 minutes of content
> - Spend $100 on paid ads

Do this every single day for 100 days without stopping or switching strategies. At the end of 100 days, you will have leads. Most people quit before they hit critical mass.

**Why 100 works:** It's enough volume to get statistically meaningful feedback, enough reps to develop skill, and enough consistency to build momentum. Below 100/day, results are too sporadic to learn from.

## THE LEAD GETTERS (Scaling Through Others)

Once you've mastered the Core Four yourself, you scale by getting OTHER people to do lead generation for you. There are four types of Lead Getters:

### 1. Customer Referrals
Your best customers become your best marketers. But you must ASK systematically — don't wait for it to happen organically.

**Referral Offer Framework:** Treat referrals like an offer. Give the referrer something valuable. Give the referred person something valuable. Make it easy to execute.

- Ask at the point of peak satisfaction (just got a result, just had a win)
- Make the referral mechanism frictionless (link, code, introduction template)
- Reward both parties (the referrer AND the new person)

### 2. Employees
Hire people to do the Core Four activities for you. Each employee becomes a lead generation machine executing your proven playbook.

- Train them on YOUR proven scripts, YOUR proven content, YOUR proven ads
- Don't hire until you've personally proven the channel works
- First marketing hire executes your playbook — they don't create strategy

### 3. Agencies
Pay external companies to execute one or more Core Four activities. Agencies bring expertise and speed.

- Only hire agencies for channels you understand (so you can evaluate their work)
- Set clear KPIs: cost per lead, cost per engaged lead, cost per customer
- Agencies are a leverage tool, not a substitute for understanding your own marketing

### 4. Affiliates
Other businesses or individuals promote your offer to THEIR audience in exchange for commission or reciprocal benefit.

- **Affiliate Offer:** What you give affiliates to promote (usually your lead magnet + commission on sales)
- **Affiliate Recruitment:** Treat finding affiliates like its own sales process
- **Affiliate Support:** Give them proven creative, scripts, and landing pages — don't make them create from scratch
- Best affiliates already serve your ideal customer with a non-competing product

## MORE, BETTER, NEW (The Scaling Framework)

The universal framework for growing any marketing channel. Applied in strict order:

### 1. MORE
Do more of what's already working. If warm outreach generates leads, do MORE warm outreach. If content works, post MORE content. Double the volume before changing anything else.

### 2. BETTER
Once you've maxed out "more," optimize what you're doing. Better scripts, better hooks, better targeting, better creative. Systematic A/B testing. Improve conversion at each step.

### 3. NEW
Only after maxing out "more" and "better" should you try something new:
- **New placements** on existing platforms (e.g., YouTube Shorts if you're already doing YouTube long-form)
- **Known placements on new platforms** (e.g., apply your proven format to TikTok)
- **New Core Four activities** (e.g., add cold outreach if you've only been doing content)

**The Constraint Question:** "What's stopping them from doing 10x what they're currently doing?" The answer reveals whether you need more, better, or new.

## THE ADVERTISING MATH

### Engaged Leads Per Advertising Activity
```
Engaged Leads = Volume of Activity x Conversion Rate to Engaged Lead
```

### Revenue Per Advertising Activity
```
Revenue = Engaged Leads x Close Rate x Average Revenue Per Customer
```

### The Compounding Effect
Content compounds. Each new piece lives forever and continues generating leads indefinitely. After 1,000 posts, you have 1,000 assets working for you 24/7. This is why content creators who persist for years seem to "blow up overnight."

## KEY LEADS PRINCIPLES

1. **Advertising is a moral obligation** — if you have something that helps people, not advertising is selfish. You're keeping the solution from the people who need it.
2. **The lead magnet IS the first impression of your brand** — most people will never buy, but they will judge you based on the free stuff. Make it exceptional.
3. **Play games where if you wait, you win** — lead generation compounds. The longer you persist, the more unfair your advantage becomes.
4. **One channel, one offer, maximum volume** — don't spread across five channels at 20% effort each. Pick one channel, go 100%.
5. **Track the right metrics** — not impressions, not clicks. Track cost per engaged lead and cost per customer.
6. **Speed to lead matters** — the faster you respond to an engaged lead, the higher the conversion rate. Minutes, not hours.
